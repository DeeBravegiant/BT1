# Q564: cb-mpc protocol party-name aliasing in base_hash.h

## Question
Can an unprivileged attacker enter through `coinbase::api::tdh2::combine_ac` with access_structure, party_names, public_shares, label, partial names, partial decryptions, and ciphertext while two sessions run concurrently, reach `include-internal/cbmpc/internal/crypto/base_hash.h` `base_hash module`, and use duplicate, reordered, empty, or colliding party_names and quorum_party_names to bypass the requirement that party names map one-to-one to stable pids and access-structure leaves, causing a below-threshold set is treated as a valid quorum or share owner and producing an in-scope cb-mpc bounty impact?

- Attacker controls: access_structure, party_names, public_shares, label, partial names, partial decryptions, and ciphertext; specifically duplicate, reordered, empty, or colliding party_names and quorum_party_names while two sessions run concurrently

The malicious side supplies duplicate, reordered, empty, or colliding party_names and quorum_party_names.
