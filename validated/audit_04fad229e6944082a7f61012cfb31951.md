### Title
Resigned Committee Member's `CommitteeMemberResigned` Status Persists After Re-election Without Prior Removal, Permanently Blocking Hot Key Authorization — (File: `eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/GovCert.hs`)

---

### Summary

In the Conway era `GOVCERT` rule, once a committee member submits a `ConwayResignCommitteeColdKey` certificate, their cold credential is permanently marked `CommitteeMemberResigned` in `CommitteeState`. If the same cold credential is subsequently re-added to the committee via an `UpdateCommittee` governance action **without first being explicitly removed**, the `updateCommitteeState` function in the `EPOCH` rule preserves the `CommitteeMemberResigned` entry. The `checkAndOverwriteCommitteeMemberState` guard then permanently blocks the re-elected member from authorizing a hot key, making them unable to vote. The committee is therefore effectively smaller than the enacted governance action intended, potentially dropping below `committeeMinSize` and causing all proposals requiring committee approval to fail ratification.

---

### Finding Description

**Root cause 1 — `GovCert.hs`, `checkAndOverwriteCommitteeMemberState`:**

The helper used for both `ConwayAuthCommitteeHotKey` and `ConwayResignCommitteeColdKey` certificates checks whether the cold credential has ever resigned:

```haskell
coldCredResigned =
  Map.lookup coldCred csCommitteeCreds >>= \case
    CommitteeMemberResigned {} -> Just coldCred
    CommitteeHotCredential {} -> Nothing
failOnJust coldCredResigned $ injectFailure . ConwayCommitteeHasPreviouslyResigned
``` [1](#0-0) 

This check fires unconditionally: if `CommitteeMemberResigned` is present in `CommitteeState`, the member can never re-authorize a hot key, regardless of whether they have since been re-elected.

**Root cause 2 — `Epoch.hs`, `updateCommitteeState`:**

At every epoch boundary, `CommitteeState` is pruned to keep only credentials that are still in the current `Committee`:

```haskell
updateCommitteeState committee (CommitteeState creds) =
  CommitteeState $ Map.intersection creds members
  where
    members = foldMap' committeeMembers committee
``` [2](#0-1) 

This function only **removes** entries for members no longer in the committee. It does **not** clear the `CommitteeMemberResigned` flag for members who have been re-added. If a resigned member is re-added to `committeeMembers` via `UpdateCommittee` (without first being removed), their `CommitteeMemberResigned` entry survives the intersection because they are still present in `members`.

**Interaction with `ConflictingCommitteeUpdate`:**

The `GOV` rule prevents a credential from appearing in both `membersToRemove` and `membersToAdd` of the same `UpdateCommittee` proposal:

```haskell
let conflicting = Set.intersection (Map.keysSet membersToAdd) membersToRemove
 in failOnNonEmptySet conflicting (injectFailure . ConflictingCommitteeUpdate)
``` [3](#0-2) 

This means the only way to clear a resignation is via **two separate** `UpdateCommittee` governance actions: one to remove the member, one to re-add them. A single `UpdateCommittee` that simply adds the resigned credential back (without removing it) is accepted by the ledger but silently fails to restore voting ability.

**`activeCommitteeSize` impact:**

The `votingCommitteeThresholdInternal` function counts only members whose `CommitteeState` entry is not `CommitteeMemberResigned`:

```haskell
isActive coldKey validUntil =
  case Map.lookup coldKey hotKeys of
    Just (CommitteeMemberResigned _) -> False
    Just _ -> currentEpoch <= validUntil
    Nothing -> False
activeCommitteeSize =
  fromIntegral . Map.size . Map.filterWithKey isActive $
    foldMap' committeeMembers committee
``` [4](#0-3) 

A re-elected but still-resigned member is not counted as active. If `activeCommitteeSize < committeeMinSize`, the committee threshold becomes `NoVotingThreshold`, and the committee effectively votes **No** on every proposal. [5](#0-4) 

---

### Impact Explanation

A governance action (`UpdateCommittee`) that re-adds a previously resigned committee member without first removing them is accepted by the ledger but does not restore the member's ability to vote. The committee's effective size remains below what the enacted governance action intended. If this causes `activeCommitteeSize < committeeMinSize`, every governance proposal requiring committee approval (treasury withdrawals, parameter changes, constitution updates, hard-fork initiations) fails ratification. The `Committee` data structure in `EnactState` shows the member as present, but the `CommitteeState` in `CertState` permanently blocks their participation — a silent divergence between the two registries.

The `Committee` type stores members as a `Map (Credential ColdCommitteeRole) EpochNo`: [6](#0-5) 

While `CommitteeState` stores authorizations separately: [7](#0-6) 

These two registries can diverge: `Committee` says the member is elected; `CommitteeState` says they are permanently resigned.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Any committee member can unilaterally submit a `ConwayResignCommitteeColdKey` certificate. Governance participants who subsequently pass an `UpdateCommittee` to re-add the resigned member (a natural response) will find the action accepted without error, yet the committee remains non-functional for all proposals requiring committee votes. The `ConflictingCommitteeUpdate` check makes the single-action fix impossible, and the two-action workaround is non-obvious. The test suite documents this behavior as expected but does not surface it as a warning to governance participants. [8](#0-7) 

---

### Recommendation

`updateCommitteeState` should clear `CommitteeMemberResigned` entries for credentials that are present in the new `committeeMembers` map (i.e., re-added members). A targeted fix:

```haskell
updateCommitteeState committee (CommitteeState creds) =
  CommitteeState $
    Map.mapWithKey clearIfReAdded $
      Map.intersection creds members
  where
    members = foldMap' committeeMembers committee
    clearIfReAdded coldCred auth =
      case auth of
        CommitteeMemberResigned _
          | Map.member coldCred members -> CommitteeHotCredential ... -- or simply delete the entry
        _ -> auth
```

Alternatively, the `checkAndOverwriteCommitteeMemberState` guard should only block re-authorization if the member's resignation has not been superseded by a subsequent re-election (i.e., if the member was removed from `committeeMembers` and then re-added, the `CommitteeMemberResigned` entry should have been cleared at the epoch boundary).

### Proof of Concept

1. Set `committeeMinSize = 2`.
2. Elect a committee of exactly 2 members (`coldC1`, `coldC2`).
3. `coldC2` submits `ConwayResignCommitteeColdKey` → `CommitteeState` records `coldC2 → CommitteeMemberResigned`.
4. Governance passes `UpdateCommittee` with `membersToAdd = {coldC2 → epoch+10}` and `membersToRemove = {}`.
5. After enactment: `Committee.committeeMembers` contains both `coldC1` and `coldC2`; `updateCommitteeState` keeps `coldC2 → CommitteeMemberResigned` because `coldC2 ∈ members`.
6. `coldC2` submits `ConwayAuthCommitteeHotKey` → rejected with `ConwayCommitteeHasPreviouslyResigned`.
7. `activeCommitteeSize = 1 < committeeMinSize = 2` → `votingCommitteeThresholdInternal` returns `NoVotingThreshold`.
8. All treasury withdrawals, parameter changes, and constitution updates fail ratification indefinitely, until two further governance actions (remove `coldC2`, then re-add `coldC2`) are enacted. [9](#0-8) [10](#0-9)
