Audit Report

## Title
JIT Liquidity Attack Enables Fee Extraction Without Meaningful Liquidity Provision — (File: `metric-core/contracts/libraries/LiquidityLib.sol`)

## Summary
`LiquidityLib.addLiquidity` and `LiquidityLib.removeLiquidity` impose no minimum holding period, time-lock, or fee-on-exit. LP fees are credited directly into each bin's token balance the moment a swap settles via `SwapMath`. An MEV actor can sandwich any large swap — `addLiquidity` → swap → `removeLiquidity` — to capture a proportional share of the LP fee with near-zero net impermanent loss, stealing fee revenue that rightfully belongs to long-term liquidity providers.

## Finding Description
**Fee accounting root cause.** In `SwapMath.buyToken0InBinSpecifiedIn` the LP-fee portion of the input token is permanently embedded in `binState.token1BalanceScaled` at swap time:

```solidity
// SwapMath.sol L639-641
binState.token0BalanceScaled -= out0Scaled.toUint104();
binState.token1BalanceScaled =
    uint256((binState.token1BalanceScaled) + totalIn1Scaled - protocolFeeAmountScaled).toUint104();
```

`token1FeeScaled - protocolFeeAmountScaled` (the LP fee net of protocol cut) is indistinguishable from principal inside the bin balance from this point forward.

**Share-proportional withdrawal.** `removeLiquidity` pays out the full current balance proportionally:

```solidity
// LiquidityLib.sol L205-206
uint256 amount0Scaled = _checkedMul(binState.token0BalanceScaled, sharesToRemove) / binTotalSharesVal;
uint256 amount1Scaled = _checkedMul(binState.token1BalanceScaled, sharesToRemove) / binTotalSharesVal;
```

No snapshot of the balance at deposit time is taken; any fee accrued after entry is immediately claimable.

**No temporal guard.** Neither `addLiquidity` (L40–159) nor `removeLiquidity` (L161–251) records or checks `block.number` / `block.timestamp`. There is no lock, cooldown, or exit fee. The only checks are share sufficiency and `minimalMintableLiquidity`.

**Exploit path:**
1. Attacker observes a large pending swap targeting bin `b` (mempool or same-block bundle).
2. Attacker calls `addLiquidity([b], [s])`, paying `ceil(T0·s/S)` token0 and `ceil(T1·s/S)` token1. New total shares = `S + s`.
3. Swap executes; LP fee `F` is added to `binState.tokenXBalanceScaled`.
4. Attacker calls `removeLiquidity([b], [s])`, receiving `(T0'·s)/(S+s)` and `(T1'·s)/(S+s)` where `T1'` includes `F`.
5. Net gain ≈ `F·s/(S+s)` in fee-token terms; existing LPs lose `F·s/(S+s)` of owed fee assets.

## Impact Explanation
This is a direct, repeatable loss of owed LP assets. For a pool with `spreadFeeE6 = 3000` (0.3 %) and a 100 000 USDC swap, the LP fee is ≈ 300 USDC; an attacker matching existing liquidity (`s = S`) captures ≈ 150 USDC per sandwich, far exceeding L2 gas costs. The stolen amount is real token value transferred from honest long-term LPs to the attacker on every sandwiched swap. This meets the Sherlock High/Medium threshold for direct loss of owed LP assets.

## Likelihood Explanation
- Requires only mempool visibility and a standard MEV bot — no privileged role.
- Profitable on every large swap where `F·s/(S+s) > gas cost`.
- Fully automatable and scales linearly with pool volume.
- No existing on-chain guard prevents it; the `paused` flag is admin-only and unrelated.

## Recommendation
1. **Minimum holding period**: record `block.number` or `block.timestamp` per `positionBinKey` at deposit and revert `removeLiquidity` if elapsed time is below a threshold (e.g., 1 block or 60 seconds).
2. **Fee-on-remove**: charge a small exit fee that decays with holding time, making same-block round-trips unprofitable.
3. **Time-weighted fee accrual**: track a `feeGrowthInsideX` snapshot per share at deposit time (analogous to Uniswap v3's `feeGrowthInside`) and only distribute fees accrued after the LP's entry, preventing retroactive fee capture.

## Proof of Concept
**Setup:** Bin 0: `token0BalanceScaled = 1 000 000`, `token1BalanceScaled = 1 000 000`, `binTotalShares = 1 000 000`. Oracle mid-price = 1:1. `spreadFeeE6 = 3000`. Protocol fee share = 0.

**Step 1 — Attacker adds 1 000 000 shares:**
- Pays `ceil(1 000 000 · 1 000 000 / 1 000 000)` = 1 000 000 token0 + 1 000 000 token1.
- New totals: token0 = 2 000 000, token1 = 2 000 000, shares = 2 000 000.

**Step 2 — Large swap: 1 000 000 token1 in, ~997 000 token0 out (0.3 % fee):**
- LP fee ≈ 3 000 token1 stays in bin.
- Bin after: token0 ≈ 1 003 000, token1 ≈ 3 003 000.

**Step 3 — Attacker removes 1 000 000 shares:**
- Receives `1 003 000 · 1 000 000 / 2 000 000` = 501 500 token0.
- Receives `3 003 000 · 1 000 000 / 2 000 000` = 1 501 500 token1.

**Attacker net (at oracle 1:1):** Paid 2 000 000; received 2 003 000. **Profit: 3 000 tokens.**

The existing LP's fee share is halved from 3 000 to 1 500 tokens — a direct, measurable loss of owed LP assets with no on-chain remedy. A Foundry integration test can reproduce this by deploying the pool, seeding bin 0, calling `addLiquidity` → `swap` → `removeLiquidity` in sequence and asserting the attacker's net token gain equals `F·s/(S+s)`.
