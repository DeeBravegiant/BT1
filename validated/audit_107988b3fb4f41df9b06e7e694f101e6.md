Audit Report

## Title
Period-Reset Floor Arithmetic in `_checkAndUpdateDailyFeeMintLimit` Allows Two Full Daily Fee Allowances in Less Than 24 Hours — (`contracts/LRTOracle.sol`)

## Summary
When `updateRSETHPrice()` is called after the oracle has been idle for more than one full day, `_checkAndUpdateDailyFeeMintLimit` resets `feePeriodStartTime` to `getCurrentPeriodStartTime()`, which floors to the last 24-hour boundary rather than `block.timestamp`. This places the new period's start in the past, shortening the time until the next reset. A caller can therefore trigger two full `maxFeeMintAmountPerDay` fee mints in as little as 23 hours, causing the treasury to receive more rsETH than the daily cap was designed to allow and diluting rsETH holders' yield.

## Finding Description
`updateRSETHPrice()` is a public, permissionless function: [1](#0-0) 

It calls `_checkAndUpdateDailyFeeMintLimit`, which resets the period when `block.timestamp >= feePeriodStartTime + 1 days`: [2](#0-1) 

The reset assigns `feePeriodStartTime = getCurrentPeriodStartTime()`: [3](#0-2) 

`getCurrentPeriodStartTime()` computes `feePeriodStartTime + floor((block.timestamp - feePeriodStartTime) / 1 days) * 1 days`. When the oracle has been idle for `24h + X` (X > 0), integer division floors `daysElapsed` to 1, returning `feePeriodStartTime + 24h` — a timestamp **X seconds in the past**. The next period boundary therefore arrives in `24h − X` seconds, not 24 hours.

**Concrete trace (X = 1 hour):**

| Time | Action | `feePeriodStartTime` | `currentPeriodMintedFeeAmount` |
|---|---|---|---|
| T0 | Period initialized | T0 | 0 |
| T0 + 25h | Call 1: `updateRSETHPrice()` | T0 + 24h (= now − 1h) | `maxFeeMintAmountPerDay` |
| T0 + 48h (Call 1 + 23h) | Call 2: `updateRSETHPrice()` | T0 + 48h | `maxFeeMintAmountPerDay` |

Two full daily allowances are consumed in **23 hours** from Call 1. The fee minting path that executes this: [4](#0-3) 

The existing cap check (`currentPeriodMintedFeeAmount + feeAmount > maxFeeMintAmountPerDay`) is insufficient because it only enforces the cap within a single period — it does not prevent the period from being reset prematurely due to the floor arithmetic. [5](#0-4) 

## Impact Explanation
Each call mints up to `maxFeeMintAmountPerDay` rsETH to the treasury. By triggering two resets in less than 24 hours, the treasury receives up to `2 × maxFeeMintAmountPerDay` rsETH in a window shorter than intended. This dilutes existing rsETH holders' proportional claim on the underlying ETH TVL faster than the daily cap was designed to allow — constituting **theft of unclaimed yield from rsETH holders**.

**Impact: High — Theft of unclaimed yield.**

## Likelihood Explanation
- `updateRSETHPrice()` is public and requires no role or privilege.
- The precondition (oracle idle for >24h) is realistic: the function has no keeper incentive, and any network disruption, gas spike, or simply no one calling it for a day satisfies it.
- The TVL increase required for fee minting (`totalETHInProtocol > previousTVL`) is met naturally by staking rewards accumulating over time.
- No admin compromise, front-running, or brute force is needed.
- The attack is repeatable: any subsequent idle gap of >24h re-enables the same shortcut.

**Likelihood: Medium** (requires a >24h idle gap, which is an operational condition rather than a guaranteed state, but is plausible in production).

## Recommendation
Reset `feePeriodStartTime` to `block.timestamp` rather than to the floored past boundary. The simplest fix in `_checkAndUpdateDailyFeeMintLimit`:

```solidity
if (block.timestamp >= feePeriodStartTime + 1 days) {
    currentPeriodMintedFeeAmount = 0;
-   feePeriodStartTime = getCurrentPeriodStartTime();
+   feePeriodStartTime = block.timestamp;
}
```

This ensures each new period always starts at the moment of the first post-idle call, so the next reset is always at least 24 hours away. If calendar-aligned periods are desired, the contract should track the last-minted period index and enforce that at most one full allowance is consumed per calendar day, regardless of when the call arrives.

## Proof of Concept
**Minimal call sequence (no fork required, verifiable by inspection):**

1. Deploy with `feePeriodStartTime = T0`, `maxFeeMintAmountPerDay = CAP`.
2. Warp to `T0 + 25h`. Ensure TVL has increased by at least `CAP * newRsETHPrice` (25h of staking rewards).
3. Call `updateRSETHPrice()`. Verify: `feePeriodStartTime == T0 + 24h` (set 1 hour in the past), `currentPeriodMintedFeeAmount == CAP`, treasury balance increased by `CAP`.
4. Assert `feePeriodStartTime + 1 days == T0 + 48h == block.timestamp + 23h` — the next reset is only 23 hours away.
5. Warp to `T0 + 48h` (23 hours after step 3). Ensure TVL has increased again (23h of staking rewards ≥ CAP).
6. Call `updateRSETHPrice()`. Verify: treasury balance increased by another `CAP`.
7. Total: `2 × CAP` minted in 23 hours (from step 3 to step 6), violating the 24-hour invariant.

The key assertion at step 4 — `feePeriodStartTime + 1 days == block.timestamp + 23 hours` — is directly derivable from the floor arithmetic in `getCurrentPeriodStartTime()` and requires no external dependencies to verify.
