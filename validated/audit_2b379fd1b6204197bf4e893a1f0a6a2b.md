### Title
Instantaneous `InstantStake` Snapshot Used for DRep Voting-Power Calculation Enables Last-Block Governance Manipulation - (File: `eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Governance.hs`)

---

### Summary

In the Conway era, the DRep stake distribution that determines whether governance actions are ratified is computed from the live `InstantStake` captured at the exact epoch boundary. An unprivileged actor who holds a large UTxO with a staking credential delegated to a DRep they control only needs to keep that UTxO unspent through the last block of an epoch. Once the epoch-boundary snapshot is taken, the actor can immediately spend the UTxO in the first block of the new epoch, yet the inflated DRep voting power persists inside the `DRepPulser` for the entire following epoch's ratification run. This is the direct Cardano analog of the Beanstalk "instantaneous BDV at Sunrise" vulnerability.

---

### Finding Description

**Vulnerability class:** Instantaneous-value snapshot manipulation affecting a periodic distribution calculation.

**Root cause — `setFreshDRepPulsingState`**

At every epoch boundary the `EPOCH` transition rule calls:

```
liftSTS $ setFreshDRepPulsingState eNo stakePoolDistr epochState2
``` [1](#0-0) 

Inside `setFreshDRepPulsingState`, the live `InstantStake` is read directly from the UTxO state and frozen into the new `DRepPulser`:

```haskell
instantStake = utxoState ^. instantStakeG
...
dpInstantStake = instantStake   -- used as part of the snapshot
``` [2](#0-1) 

`InstantStake` is the per-credential coin map that is updated in lock-step with every UTxO change throughout the epoch. It reflects the state of the UTxO at the precise moment the epoch boundary is processed — including any outputs added in the very last block of the epoch. [3](#0-2) 

**How the snapshot propagates to ratification**

`dpInstantStake` is stored inside `DRepPulser` and passed unchanged to `computeDRepDistr`, which builds `reDRepDistr` — the per-DRep coin map:

```haskell
mInstantStake = Map.lookup stakeCred instantStakeCredentials
stakeAndDeposits = fold $ mInstantStake <> mProposalDeposit
...
updatedDistr = Map.insertWith (<>) dRep (stakeAndDeposits <> balance) distr
``` [4](#0-3) 

`reDRepDistr` is then consumed by `dRepAcceptedRatio` to decide whether a governance action clears its threshold:

```haskell
dRepAcceptedRatio RatifyEnv {reDRepDistr, reDRepState, reCurrentEpoch} gasDRepVotes govAction =
  toInteger yesStake %? toInteger totalExcludingAbstainStake
  where
    (yesStake, totalExcludingAbstainStake) = Map.foldlWithKey' accumStake (0, 0) reDRepDistr
``` [5](#0-4) 

This covers every governance action type: `NoConfidence`, `UpdateCommittee`, `NewConstitution`, `HardForkInitiation`, `ParameterChange`, and `TreasuryWithdrawals`.

**Contrast with the pool-reward system**

The Shelley/Conway pool-reward system deliberately uses the `ssStakeGo` snapshot — two full epochs old — for reward calculation, precisely to prevent last-block manipulation. The DRep system has no equivalent delay; it uses the freshest possible value (`ssStakeMark`-equivalent, i.e., the current epoch boundary). [6](#0-5) 

---

### Impact Explanation

**Impact: Critical — Unauthorized governance action enacted.**

An attacker who can inflate a target DRep's `reDRepDistr` entry above the required voting threshold for one epoch can cause any of the following to be ratified without genuine majority support:

- Treasury withdrawals (direct ADA extraction)
- Protocol parameter changes (fee, deposit, script-execution-unit limits)
- Hard-fork initiations
- Constitutional committee replacement or no-confidence motions
- Constitution replacement

Because the `DRepPulser` snapshot is immutable once created, the inflated voting power persists for the entire epoch even after the attacker's UTxO is spent. The ledger state transition that enacts the ratified action is irreversible without a hard fork.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Likelihood: Medium.**

Prerequisites:
1. The attacker must have a registered staking credential delegated to a DRep they control (trivially achievable in any prior epoch).
2. The attacker must hold enough ADA to shift the DRep ratio past the relevant threshold (e.g., `dvtTreasuryWithdrawal`, `dvtHardForkInitiation`). The required amount depends on the current DRep distribution.
3. The ADA must be held only through the last block of the target epoch — a window of ~20 seconds on mainnet. The attacker can spend it in the very next block.

There are no flash-loan primitives on Cardano, so the attacker must own or borrow the ADA through off-chain means. However, the capital is only at risk for a single block slot, making the opportunity cost very low relative to the governance outcome being targeted. A well-funded actor (exchange, large stake pool operator, whale) targeting a specific governance action faces a realistic attack surface.

---

### Recommendation

Replace the live `instantStake` read in `setFreshDRepPulsingState` with the already-computed `ssStakeMark` snapshot that the `SNAP` rule produces at the same epoch boundary, or — mirroring the pool-reward design — use the `ssStakeSet` or `ssStakeGo` snapshot (one or two epochs old) so that the DRep distribution cannot be shifted by a single last-block transaction. Concretely, in `setFreshDRepPulsingState`, derive `dpInstantStake` from `snapshots1` (the output of the `SNAP` sub-transition already computed earlier in `epochTransition`) rather than from the live `utxoState ^. instantStakeG`. [7](#0-6)

### Proof of Concept

**Setup (any epoch N−1 or earlier):**
- Attacker registers staking credential `C` and delegates it to DRep `D` (attacker-controlled).
- DRep `D` votes `Yes` on a pending governance action `G` (e.g., a `TreasuryWithdrawals` action that would otherwise fail because `D`'s stake is below the threshold).

**Last block of epoch N:**
- Attacker submits a transaction creating a large UTxO output addressed to a payment key with staking reference `C`. This increases `instantStake[C]` by the output's coin value.
- The transaction is included in the last block of epoch N.

**Epoch N → N+1 boundary (EPOCH rule fires):**
- `setFreshDRepPulsingState` reads `utxoState ^. instantStakeG`, which now includes the large output.
- `dpInstantStake` is frozen with the inflated value.
- `computeDRepDistr` assigns the inflated coin to DRep `D` in `reDRepDistr`.

**First block of epoch N+1:**
- Attacker spends the large UTxO output. The live `instantStake` drops back to normal.
- The `DRepPulser` snapshot is unaffected; `dpInstantStake` still holds the inflated value.

**During epoch N+1 (pulsing and ratification):**
- `dRepAcceptedRatio` evaluates `reDRepDistr` with the inflated DRep `D` stake.
- Governance action `G` clears the `dvtTreasuryWithdrawal` threshold and is enacted at the next epoch boundary.
- Treasury funds are transferred to the attacker's reward account.

computeDRepDistr instantStake regDReps proposalDeposits poolDistr dRepDistr =
  Map.foldlWithKey' go (dRepDistr, poolDistr)
  where
    go (!drepAccum, !poolAccum) stakeCred accountState =
      let instantStakeCredentials = instantStake ^. instantStakeCredentialsL
          mInstantStake = Map.lookup stakeCred instantStakeCredentials
          mProposalDeposit = Map.lookup stakeCred proposalDeposits
          stakeAndDeposits = fold $ mInstantStake <> mProposalDeposit
       in ( addToDRepDistr accountState stakeAndDeposits drepAccum
          , addToPoolDistr accountState mProposalDeposit poolAccum
          )
    addToPoolDistr accountState mProposalDeposit distr = fromMaybe distr $ do
      stakePool <- accountState ^. stakePoolDelegationAccountStateL
      proposalDeposit <- mProposalDeposit
      ips <- Map.lookup stakePool $ distr ^. poolDistrDistrL
      pure $
        distr
          & poolDistrDistrL %~ Map.insert stakePool (ips & individualTotalPoolStakeL <>~ proposalDeposit)
          & poolDistrTotalL %~ \t -> unsafeNonZero (unNonZero t <> fromCompact proposalDeposit)
    addToDRepDistr accountState stakeAndDeposits distr = fromMaybe distr $ do
      dRep <- accountState ^. dRepDelegationAccountStateL
      let
        balance = accountState ^. balanceAccountStateL
        updatedDistr = Map.insertWith (<>) dRep (stakeAndDeposits <> balance) distr
      Just $ case dRep of
        DRepAlwaysAbstain -> updatedDistr
        DRepAlwaysNoConfidence -> updatedDistr
        DRepCredential cred
          -- TODO: Potential optimization. Avoid this membership check, since delegation is
          -- guaranteed to exist. I believe it would also be safe for PV9, but we need to verify
          -- that it is in fact true due to #4772
          | Map.member cred regDReps -> updatedDistr
          | otherwise -> distr

```haskell
dRepAcceptedRatio ::
  forall era.
  RatifyEnv era ->
  Map (Credential DRepRole) Vote ->
  GovAction era ->
  Rational
dRepAcceptedRatio RatifyEnv {reDRepDistr, reDRepState, reCurrentEpoch} gasDRepVotes govAction =
  toInteger yesStake %? toInteger totalExcludingAbstainStake
  where
    accumStake (!yes, !tot) drep (CompactCoin stake) =
      case drep of
        DRepCredential cred ->
          case Map.lookup cred reDRepState of
            Nothing -> (yes, tot) -- drep is not registered, so we don't consider it
            Just drepState
              | reCurrentEpoch > drepExpiry drepState -> (yes, tot) -- drep is expired, so we don't consider it
              | otherwise ->
                  case Map.lookup cred gasDRepVotes of
                    -- drep hasn't voted for this action, so we don't count
                    -- the vote but we consider it in the denominator:
                    Nothing -> (yes, tot + stake)
                    Just VoteYes -> (yes + stake, tot + stake)
                    Just Abstain -> (yes, tot)
                    Just VoteNo -> (yes, tot + stake)
        DRepAlwaysNoConfidence ->
          case govAction of
            NoConfidence _ -> (yes + stake, tot + stake)
            _ -> (yes, tot + stake)
        DRepAlwaysAbstain -> (yes, tot)
    (yesStake, totalExcludingAbstainStake) = Map.foldlWithKey' accumStake (0, 0) reDRepDistr
```
