### Title
Stale Reverse DRep Delegation in Bootstrap Phase Allows Attacker to Nullify Delegators' Current Vote Delegation — (`eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/GovCert.hs`)

---

### Summary

During the Conway bootstrap phase (protocol version 9), when a stake credential redelegates its vote from DRep A to DRep B, the stale reverse-delegation entry in DRep A's `drepDelegs` set is intentionally not removed. When DRep A is subsequently unregistered via `ConwayUnRegDRep`, the `clearDRepDelegations` helper uses the stale `drepDelegs` set to zero out the `dRepDelegation` field of every listed account — including accounts that have already moved their delegation to DRep B. The result is that those delegators' current, valid vote delegation is silently erased, removing their governance voting power without their knowledge or consent.

---

### Finding Description

**Root cause — stale reverse delegation not removed during bootstrap phase**

In `processDelegationInternal` (`eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/Deleg.hs`), when `preserveIncorrectDelegation` is `True` (i.e., `pvMajor pv < natVersion @10`, the bootstrap phase), a redelegation to a new DRep only *adds* the stake credential to the new DRep's `drepDelegs` set; it does **not** remove it from the old DRep's `drepDelegs` set:

```haskell
Just dRepCred
  -- 2) in the bootstrap phase, in order to preserve the incorrect behavior,
  --    where old reverse delegation for the prior DRep was wrongfully retained.
  | isNothing mAccountState || preserveIncorrectDelegation ->
      certVStateL . vsDRepsL
        %~ Map.adjust (drepDelegsL %~ Set.insert stakeCred) dRepCred
``` [1](#0-0) 

After this operation, DRep A's `drepDelegs` still contains the stake credential even though the credential's forward delegation (`dRepDelegationAccountStateL`) now points to DRep B.

**Root cause — `ConwayUnRegDRep` blindly clears all entries in `drepDelegs`**

When DRep A is unregistered, the `ConwayUnRegDRep` branch in `conwayGovCertTransition` calls `clearDRepDelegations` using the stale `drepDelegs` set:

```haskell
let
  certState' =
    certState & certVStateL . vsDRepsL %~ Map.delete cred
  clearDRepDelegations delegs accountsMap =
    foldr (Map.adjust (dRepDelegationAccountStateL .~ Nothing)) accountsMap delegs
pure $
  case mDRepState of
    Nothing -> certState'
    Just dRepState ->
      certState'
        & certDStateL . accountsL . accountsMapL
          %~ clearDRepDelegations (drepDelegs dRepState)
``` [2](#0-1) 

`clearDRepDelegations` sets `dRepDelegationAccountStateL .~ Nothing` for **every** credential in `drepDelegs`, with no check of whether the credential is still actually delegated to this DRep. Because `drepDelegs` contains stale entries from the bootstrap-phase redelegation bug, credentials that have already moved to DRep B have their `dRepDelegation` field incorrectly set to `Nothing`.

**Confirmed by an existing test**

The test suite explicitly confirms this behavior in bootstrap mode:

```haskell
impAnn "Check that unregistration of previous delegation does not affect current delegation" $ do
  unRegisterDRep drepCred
  ifBootstrap
    ( do
        accounts <- getsNES $ nesEsL . esLStateL . lsCertStateL . certDStateL . accountsL
        expectNothingExpr (lookupDRepDelegation cred accounts)   -- delegation is gone!
        expecteReverseDRepDelegation cred drepCred2 True
    )
    (expectDelegatedVote cred (DRepCredential drepCred2))
``` [3](#0-2) 

In bootstrap phase, after `drepCred` (the old DRep) is unregistered, `lookupDRepDelegation cred accounts` returns `Nothing` — the delegator's current delegation to `drepCred2` (the new DRep) has been silently erased.

---

### Impact Explanation

**Governance voting power is silently nullified outside design parameters.**

After the attack, affected delegators have `dRepDelegation = Nothing`. Their stake no longer counts toward any DRep's voting power. This:

- Reduces the effective voting stake of DRep B (the legitimate target DRep) without any action by DRep B or its delegators.
- Can prevent governance proposals from reaching the required DRep threshold, or conversely allow proposals to pass that would otherwise fail, depending on which DReps are targeted.
- Persists until the delegator actively re-delegates, which they may not know is necessary.

This matches the allowed impact: **Medium — attacker-controlled transactions modify governance participation outside design parameters**, with potential escalation to **Critical — unauthorized governance action is enacted** if the attack is applied at sufficient scale to tip ratification thresholds.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The attack requires only unprivileged on-chain actions available to any participant:

1. Register as a DRep (`ConwayRegDRep` certificate) — no special role required.
2. Attract delegations from stake credentials (or simply wait; many delegators may have previously delegated to the attacker's DRep and then moved on).
3. Wait for delegators to redelegate to a different DRep during bootstrap phase (PV9).
4. Submit `ConwayUnRegDRep` to unregister — this triggers `clearDRepDelegations` with the stale set.

The bootstrap phase (PV9) is the current/recent phase on mainnet. The attack is cheap (one transaction), reversible only by the victim re-delegating, and leaves no on-chain evidence of malicious intent.

---

### Recommendation

The `clearDRepDelegations` function in `ConwayUnRegDRep` must verify that each credential in `drepDelegs` is still actually delegated to the DRep being unregistered before clearing it. A corrected version:

```haskell
clearDRepDelegations delegs accountsMap =
  foldr
    ( Map.adjust
        ( \acctState ->
            if acctState ^. dRepDelegationAccountStateL == Just (DRepCredential cred)
              then acctState & dRepDelegationAccountStateL .~ Nothing
              else acctState
        )
    )
    accountsMap
    delegs
```

Alternatively, the stale reverse delegation should be removed at the point of redelegation even in bootstrap phase, which is what the `updateDRepDelegations` migration at PV10 already does retroactively. [4](#0-3) 

The PV10 migration confirms the intended correct state: `drepDelegs` should only contain credentials that are currently delegated to that DRep. The fix should enforce this invariant at the point of unregistration, not only at the hard-fork boundary.

---

### Proof of Concept

```
1. Bootstrap phase (PV9) is active.

2. Alice registers stake credential `cred` and delegates vote to DRep A (`drepCredA`).
   State: drepDelegs(A) = {cred}, cred.dRepDelegation = DRepCredential drepCredA

3. Alice redelegates vote to DRep B (`drepCredB`).
   State (bootstrap bug): drepDelegs(A) = {cred}  ← stale, not removed
                           drepDelegs(B) = {cred}
                           cred.dRepDelegation = DRepCredential drepCredB

4. Attacker (owner of DRep A) submits UnRegDRepTxCert drepCredA.
   ConwayUnRegDRep fires clearDRepDelegations(drepDelegs(A)) = clearDRepDelegations({cred}).
   Sets cred.dRepDelegation = Nothing.

5. Final state: cred.dRepDelegation = Nothing
   Alice's vote no longer counts toward DRep B.
   DRep B's effective voting stake is reduced without Alice's knowledge.
```

The existing test at `Test/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Imp/DelegSpec.hs:313–323` reproduces this exact sequence and asserts `lookupDRepDelegation cred accounts == Nothing` in bootstrap mode, confirming the vulnerability is reachable and produces the described incorrect state. processDelegationInternal ::
  ConwayEraCertState era =>
  -- | Preserve the buggy behavior where DRep delegations are not updated correctly (See #4772)
  Bool ->
  -- | Delegator
  Credential Staking ->
  -- | Account state for the above stake credential
  Maybe (AccountState era) ->
  -- | New delegatee
  Delegatee ->
  CertState era ->
  CertState era
processDelegationInternal preserveIncorrectDelegation stakeCred mAccountState newDelegatee =
  case newDelegatee of
    DelegStake sPool -> delegStake sPool
    DelegVote dRep -> delegVote dRep
    DelegStakeVote sPool dRep -> delegVote dRep . delegStake sPool
  where
    delegStake stakePool cState =
      cState
        & certDStateL . accountsL
          %~ adjustAccountState (stakePoolDelegationAccountStateL ?~ stakePool) stakeCred
        & maybe
          (certPStateL . psStakePoolsL %~ Map.adjust (spsDelegatorsL %~ Set.insert stakeCred) stakePool)
          (\accountState -> certPStateL %~ unDelegReDelegStakePool stakeCred accountState (Just stakePool))
          mAccountState

    delegVote dRep cState =
      let handleReverseDelegation =
            case dRepToCred dRep of
              Just dRepCred
                -- This is the case where we only add the new reverse delegation and do not remove
                -- the old one, which is the behavior that we want:
                --
                -- 1) for new accounts, since there is no old reverse delegation to remove
                --
                -- 2) in the bootstrap phase, in order to preserve the incorrect behavior, where old reverse
                --   delegation for the prior DRep was wrongfully retained. It is important to note
                --   that in case when the new delegation was to a predefined DRep, the reverse
                --   delegations where handled correctly even in the boostrap phase
                --
                -- For reference here is the original bug report:
                --   https://github.com/IntersectMBO/cardano-ledger/issues/4772
                | isNothing mAccountState || preserveIncorrectDelegation ->
                    certVStateL . vsDRepsL
                      %~ Map.adjust (drepDelegsL %~ Set.insert stakeCred) dRepCred
              _
                -- AccountState existed before this delegation, therefore we need to properly handle
                -- potential undelegation of the old DRep
                | Just accountState <- mAccountState ->
                    certVStateL %~ unDelegReDelegDRep stakeCred accountState (Just dRep)
                -- If this is a fresh registration with delegation to a predefined DRep, there are
                -- no extra steps that need to be done
                | otherwise -> id
       in cState
            & certDStateL . accountsL
              %~ adjustAccountState (dRepDelegationAccountStateL ?~ dRep) stakeCred
            & handleReverseDelegation
```

```haskell
updateDRepDelegations :: ConwayEraCertState era => CertState era -> CertState era
updateDRepDelegations certState =
  let accountsMap = certState ^. certDStateL . accountsL . accountsMapL
      dReps =
        -- Reset all delegations in order to remove any inconsistencies
        -- Delegations will be reset accordingly below.
        Map.map (\dRepState -> dRepState {drepDelegs = Set.empty}) $
          certState ^. certVStateL . vsDRepsL
      (dRepsWithDelegations, accountsWithoutUnknownDRepDelegations) =
        Map.mapAccumWithKey adjustDelegations dReps accountsMap
      adjustDelegations ds stakeCred accountState =
        case accountState ^. dRepDelegationAccountStateL of
          Just (DRepCredential dRep) ->
            let addDelegation _ dRepState =
                  Just $ dRepState {drepDelegs = Set.insert stakeCred (drepDelegs dRepState)}
             in case Map.updateLookupWithKey addDelegation dRep ds of
                  (Nothing, _) -> (ds, accountState & dRepDelegationAccountStateL .~ Nothing)
                  (Just _, ds') -> (ds', accountState)
          _ -> (ds, accountState)
   in certState
        -- Remove dangling delegations to non-existent DReps:
        & certDStateL . accountsL . accountsMapL .~ accountsWithoutUnknownDRepDelegations
        -- Populate DRep delegations with delegatees
        & certVStateL . vsDRepsL .~ dRepsWithDelegations
