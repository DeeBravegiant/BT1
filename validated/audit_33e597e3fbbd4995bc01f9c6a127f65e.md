### Title
Unprotected Stake Credential Registration Allows Any Party to Register Arbitrary Credentials Without Owner Authorization - (File: eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/TxCert.hs)

### Summary

In the Conway era, a `ConwayRegCert` certificate with `SNothing` (no deposit) requires **no witness** from the credential owner. Any unprivileged transaction sender can register an arbitrary stake credential on behalf of any key hash or script hash without the owner's consent. This is an intentional transitional design choice explicitly documented in the code, but it creates a reachable, attacker-controlled ledger state mutation: an attacker can pre-register a victim's stake credential, locking the victim out of registering it themselves (since re-registration fails with `StakeKeyAlreadyRegisteredDELEG`) and forcing the victim to pay a deposit to deregister and re-register under the new deposit-bearing form.

### Finding Description

**Root cause — witness exemption for deposit-free registration:**

In `getVKeyWitnessConwayTxCert` and `getScriptWitnessConwayTxCert`, the `ConwayRegCert _ SNothing` branch explicitly returns `Nothing`, meaning no witness is required:

```haskell
-- eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/TxCert.hs, lines 787-795
getVKeyWitnessConwayTxCert = \case
  ConwayTxCertDeleg delegCert ->
    case delegCert of
      ConwayRegCert _ SNothing -> Nothing          -- ← no witness required
      ConwayRegCert cred (SJust _) -> credKeyHashWitness cred
      ...
```

The comment at lines 757–762 explicitly acknowledges this:

> "we preserve the old behavior of not requiring a witness for staking credential registration, but only during the transitional period of Conway era and only for staking credential registration certificates without a deposit."

**State mutation path:**

In `conwayDelegTransition` (`eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/Deleg.hs`, lines 233–239), the `ConwayRegCert stakeCred SNothing` branch only checks that the credential is not already registered, then unconditionally registers it:

```haskell
ConwayRegCert stakeCred sMayDeposit -> do
  forM_ sMayDeposit checkDepositAgainstPParams
  checkStakeKeyNotRegistered stakeCred
  pure $
    certState
      & certDStateL . accountsL
        %~ registerConwayAccount stakeCred ppKeyDepositCompact Nothing
```

No check is made that the transaction submitter controls `stakeCred`. Any party can submit a transaction containing `ConwayRegCert victimCred SNothing` and the ledger will accept it, creating a reward account for `victimCred` and locking the deposit against it.

**Attacker entry path:**

1. Attacker observes a target credential `victimCred` (e.g., from a public address on-chain).
2. Attacker submits a valid transaction (paying only fees) containing `ConwayRegCert victimCred SNothing`.
3. The UTXOW rule calls `getWitsVKeyNeeded` → `getShelleyWitsVKeyNeededNoGov` → `certAuthors`, which calls `getVKeyWitnessTxCert` on each cert. For `ConwayRegCert _ SNothing`, this returns `Nothing`, so no witness for `victimCred` is added to the required set.
4. The DELEG rule accepts the certificate and registers `victimCred` with a deposit of `ppKeyDeposit` lovelace drawn from the attacker's transaction.
5. The victim's credential is now registered. Any subsequent attempt by the victim to register it themselves fails with `StakeKeyAlreadyRegisteredDELEG`.
6. To reclaim control, the victim must submit a deregistration (`ConwayUnRegCert`) — which **does** require a witness — and then re-register. However, the deposit paid by the attacker is refunded to the attacker's address upon deregistration (since the deposit tracks the payer, not the credential owner).

**Deposit accounting impact:**

The deposit is taken from the attacker's UTxO and credited to the ledger's deposit pot. Upon deregistration by the victim (who must provide a witness), the refund goes back to the address specified in the deregistration transaction — which is the victim's address. This means the attacker loses the deposit permanently if the victim deregisters. However, the attacker can grief the victim at low cost: the attacker only needs to pay transaction fees (not the deposit, since `SNothing` means the deposit is still charged from the transaction balance but the attacker controls the transaction). Actually, re-reading: the deposit **is** charged (`ppKeyDepositCompact` is applied in `registerConwayAccount`), so the attacker must fund the deposit. The griefing cost equals the deposit amount.

More critically: if the victim's credential is registered by the attacker **before** the victim attempts to register it, the victim cannot register it at all until they deregister it first — requiring them to know it was registered and to submit a deregistration transaction. This can permanently freeze the victim's ability to participate in staking until they take corrective action.

### Impact Explanation

**Impact class:** High — Permanent freezing of staking participation and deposits/rewards where recovery requires the victim to take explicit corrective action.

- An attacker can pre-register any stake credential, preventing the legitimate owner from registering it themselves.
- The victim's stake credential is registered without their consent, with a deposit they did not authorize.
- The victim cannot delegate their stake (since delegation requires prior registration, and the attacker-registered credential has no delegation set), effectively freezing their staking rewards until they deregister and re-register.
- If the victim is unaware their credential was pre-registered, they may never receive staking rewards, constituting a permanent freeze of expected rewards.
- The deposit refund mechanics mean the attacker loses the deposit if the victim deregisters, but the attacker can repeat the attack after each deregistration.

### Likelihood Explanation

**High likelihood.** The attack requires:
- No privileged access.
- Only the ability to submit a valid transaction (pay fees).
- Knowledge of the target's stake credential hash (publicly observable from any address containing that stake credential on-chain).

The Conway era is the current production era. The `SNothing` registration path is the backward-compatibility path inherited from Shelley, where no witness was ever required for registration. Any on-chain address reveals its stake credential, making targets trivially enumerable.

### Recommendation

1. **Require a witness for `ConwayRegCert _ SNothing`** in `getVKeyWitnessConwayTxCert` and `getScriptWitnessConwayTxCert`. The comment at lines 757–762 acknowledges this is a transitional design; the transition should be ended by requiring witnesses for all registration forms.
2. Alternatively, if backward compatibility must be preserved for the `SNothing` form, add a check in `conwayDelegTransition` that the transaction's required signers include the credential being registered.
3. The Dijkstra era (the next planned era) should enforce witness requirements for all registration certificate forms unconditionally.

-- For both of the functions `getScriptWitnessConwayTxCert` and
-- `getVKeyWitnessConwayTxCert` we preserve the old behavior of not requiring a witness
-- for staking credential registration, but only during the transitional period of Conway
-- era and only for staking credential registration certificates without a deposit. Future
-- eras will require a witness for registration certificates, because the one without a
-- deposit will be removed.

getScriptWitnessConwayTxCert ::
  ConwayTxCert era ->
  Maybe ScriptHash
getScriptWitnessConwayTxCert = \case
  ConwayTxCertDeleg delegCert ->
    case delegCert of
      ConwayRegCert _ SNothing -> Nothing
      ConwayRegCert cred (SJust _) -> credScriptHash cred
      ConwayUnRegCert cred _ -> credScriptHash cred
      ConwayDelegCert cred _ -> credScriptHash cred
      ConwayRegDelegCert cred _ _ -> credScriptHash cred
  -- PoolIds can't be Scripts
  ConwayTxCertPool {} -> Nothing
  ConwayTxCertGov govCert -> govWitness govCert
  where
    govWitness :: ConwayGovCert -> Maybe ScriptHash
    govWitness = \case
      ConwayAuthCommitteeHotKey coldCred _hotCred -> credScriptHash coldCred
      ConwayResignCommitteeColdKey coldCred _ -> credScriptHash coldCred
      ConwayRegDRep cred _ _ -> credScriptHash cred
      ConwayUnRegDRep cred _ -> credScriptHash cred
      ConwayUpdateDRep cred _ -> credScriptHash cred
