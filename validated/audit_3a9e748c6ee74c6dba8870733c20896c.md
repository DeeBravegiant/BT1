Audit Report

## Title
Withdrawing Users Systematically Lose Accrued Yield Due to Stale-Rate Cap in `_calculatePayoutAmount` - (File: contracts/LRTWithdrawalManager.sol)

## Summary
When `initiateWithdrawal()` is called, rsETH is transferred to `LRTWithdrawalManager` but not burned, leaving it in `totalSupply()` where it continues to participate in reward-driven price appreciation. At `unlockQueue()` time, `_calculatePayoutAmount()` enforces `min(expectedAssetAmount, currentReturn)`, capping the payout at the stale request-time rate. Any yield that accrued to the withdrawing user's rsETH during the delay window is silently redistributed to remaining rsETH holders, constituting a systematic theft of unclaimed yield from every withdrawing user.

## Finding Description

**Root cause: rsETH not burned at request time, payout capped at stale rate.**

In `initiateWithdrawal()`, the rsETH is pulled from the user and held in the contract without being burned:

```solidity
// LRTWithdrawalManager.sol L166-173
IERC20(lrtConfig.rsETH()).safeTransferFrom(msg.sender, address(this), rsETHUnstaked);
uint256 expectedAssetAmount = getExpectedAssetAmount(asset, rsETHUnstaked);
...
assetsCommitted[asset] += expectedAssetAmount;
```

`expectedAssetAmount` is fixed once at the live oracle price and stored in the `WithdrawalRequest` struct. The rsETH is **not burned**; it remains outstanding in `totalSupply()`.

**Reward accrual to pending rsETH.**

`LRTOracle._updateRsETHPrice()` computes the new price using the full outstanding supply:

```solidity
// LRTOracle.sol L216, L250
uint256 rsethSupply = IRSETH(rsETHTokenAddress).totalSupply();
...
uint256 newRsETHPrice = (totalETHInProtocol - protocolFeeInETH).divWad(rsethSupply);
```

`rsethSupply` includes the rsETH sitting in `LRTWithdrawalManager`. When EigenLayer rewards arrive and `totalETHInProtocol` grows, `rsETHPrice` rises across the entire outstanding supply — including the pending withdrawal rsETH.

**Payout capped at the stale request-time rate.**

When `unlockQueue()` is called, `_calculatePayoutAmount()` enforces:

```solidity
// LRTWithdrawalManager.sol L833-834
uint256 currentReturn = (request.rsETHUnstaked * rsETHPrice) / assetPrice;
return (request.expectedAssetAmount < currentReturn) ? request.expectedAssetAmount : currentReturn;
```

If rewards have accrued during the delay, `currentReturn > expectedAssetAmount`. The function returns `expectedAssetAmount` — the stale, lower value. The rsETH is then burned at line 305, and only `payoutAmount` (= `expectedAssetAmount`) is redeemed from the vault. The delta between `currentReturn` and `expectedAssetAmount` — the yield that accrued to the withdrawing user's rsETH — remains in the vault and is absorbed by remaining rsETH holders through the next price update.

**Asymmetry:** The withdrawing user bears full downside (if `rsETHPrice` drops, they receive `currentReturn < expectedAssetAmount`) but receives none of the upside (if `rsETHPrice` rises, they are capped at `expectedAssetAmount`). No existing check corrects this: the `min()` in `_calculatePayoutAmount` is the only guard, and it is precisely the source of the asymmetry.

## Impact Explanation

**High — Theft of unclaimed yield.**

Every withdrawing user loses the yield that accrued to their rsETH during the withdrawal delay window (default 8 days, maximum 16 days). That yield is not returned to them; it is redistributed to remaining rsETH holders via the price mechanism. The magnitude scales with the size of the pending withdrawal, the reward rate during the delay, and the length of the delay. For large withdrawals during normal protocol operation, the loss is material and repeatable.

## Likelihood Explanation

**High.** EigenLayer generates staking rewards continuously. Over an 8-day withdrawal delay, it is near-certain that `updateRSETHPrice()` will be called at least once with a higher `totalETHInProtocol`, raising `rsETHPrice` above the level at which `expectedAssetAmount` was fixed. Every user who calls `initiateWithdrawal()` during normal protocol operation is affected. No special attacker capability is required — the loss is automatic and structural.

## Recommendation

Choose one of two consistent designs:

1. **Burn rsETH at `initiateWithdrawal()` time.** Remove the burned rsETH from `totalSupply()` immediately so it no longer participates in reward accrual. The fixed `expectedAssetAmount` then correctly represents the user's entitlement with no accrual gap.

2. **Recalculate payout at `unlockQueue()` time without a cap.** Do not store `expectedAssetAmount` as a ceiling. Instead, compute the payout entirely from the current oracle prices at unlock time, so the withdrawing user receives the rate that reflects all rewards (and losses) that accrued during the delay — consistent with how remaining holders are treated.

## Proof of Concept

1. Alice holds 100 rsETH. `rsETHPrice = 1.00 ETH/rsETH`, `assetPrice(ETH) = 1e18`. She calls `initiateWithdrawal(ETH, 100e18)`.
   - `expectedAssetAmount = 100e18 * 1e18 / 1e18 = 100 ETH` stored in request.
   - 100 rsETH transferred to `LRTWithdrawalManager` at L166, **not burned**. `totalSupply()` still includes Alice's 100 rsETH.

2. Over the next 8 days, EigenLayer rewards of 10 ETH arrive. `updateRSETHPrice()` is called. `totalETHInProtocol` increases by 10 ETH. With 1000 rsETH total supply (Alice's 100 included), `rsETHPrice` rises to `1.01 ETH/rsETH` per L250 of `LRTOracle.sol`.

3. Operator calls `unlockQueue(ETH, ...)`. `_calculatePayoutAmount()` at L833-834 computes:
   - `currentReturn = 100e18 * 1.01e18 / 1e18 = 101 ETH`
   - `min(100 ETH, 101 ETH) = 100 ETH` → Alice receives 100 ETH.

4. Alice's 100 rsETH is burned at L305. The 1 ETH of rewards that accrued to her rsETH during the delay remains in the vault. It is absorbed by the remaining 900 rsETH holders on the next `updateRSETHPrice()` call.

5. Alice loses 1 ETH of yield she was entitled to. The loss scales linearly with withdrawal size and reward rate over the delay window.

**Foundry test plan:** Deploy protocol on a mainnet fork. Have Alice call `initiateWithdrawal`. Advance 8 days. Simulate EigenLayer reward accrual by increasing the underlying asset balance in the NodeDelegator/strategy. Call `updateRSETHPrice()` to confirm `rsETHPrice` has risen. Call `unlockQueue`. Assert that Alice's `expectedAssetAmount` in the request equals the original stale value, not the current higher value. Assert that `currentReturn > payoutAmount` to confirm the yield delta is non-zero and retained in the vault.
