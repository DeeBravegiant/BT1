Audit Report

## Title
Permissionless `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` + `LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()` Enables MEV Reward Front-Running to Steal Yield from rsETH Holders — (`contracts/FeeReceiver.sol`, `contracts/LRTOracle.sol`)

## Summary
`FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` carries no access-control modifier, allowing any caller to inject accumulated MEV/EL rewards into `LRTDepositPool` at will. Because `LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()` is equally permissionless and deposit minting uses the last *stored* `rsETHPrice`, an attacker can deposit at the pre-reward price, atomically trigger reward injection and a price update, and exit with a pro-rata share of yield they did not earn — directly diluting the yield owed to existing rsETH holders.

## Finding Description
**Root cause — three permissionless state transitions with no ordering enforcement:**

1. `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` has no role check. Any EOA can call it, transferring the full ETH balance to `LRTDepositPool` via `receiveFromRewardReceiver()`. [1](#0-0) 

2. `LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()` is guarded only by `whenNotPaused`. Any unprivileged caller can trigger a price update. [2](#0-1) 

3. `getRsETHAmountToMint` divides by the *stored* `lrtOracle.rsETHPrice()`, not a freshly computed value. A deposit made before `sendFunds()` is priced at the pre-reward rate. [3](#0-2) 

**Exploit path:**

- `_getTotalEthInProtocol()` sums `address(this).balance` of `LRTDepositPool` (among other sources). After `sendFunds()` moves reward ETH there, TVL rises and `newRsETHPrice` rises accordingly. [4](#0-3) [5](#0-4) 

**Attack sequence (single transaction for steps 1–3):**
1. Observe `FeeReceiver.balance` accumulating MEV rewards.
2. Call `LRTDepositPool.depositETH()` — rsETH minted at stale price P₁.
3. Call `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` — reward ETH enters deposit pool, TVL rises.
4. Call `LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()` — stored price updates to P₂ > P₁.
5. After `withdrawalDelayBlocks` (~8 days), redeem rsETH at P₂ — profit = attacker's pro-rata share of the injected reward.

**Why existing guards are insufficient:**

The `pricePercentageLimit` check reverts non-manager callers only when the price increase exceeds the configured threshold. [6](#0-5) 

This fails to protect in three cases: (a) `pricePercentageLimit == 0` (unset), in which case the condition `pricePercentageLimit > 0` is false and no revert occurs; (b) the reward is small enough to stay under the threshold; (c) the attacker repeats the attack across many small reward accumulations. The withdrawal delay does not prevent the attack — it only defers profit realization.

## Impact Explanation
Every rsETH holder who held through the MEV reward accrual period loses a fraction of their yield. The attacker's deposit, priced before the reward, entitles them to a share of accumulated yield they did not earn. This is a direct, quantifiable **theft of unclaimed yield** (High severity per the allowed impact scope), proportional to the attacker's deposit size relative to total rsETH supply at the time of the price update.

## Likelihood Explanation
High. Both `sendFunds()` and `updateRSETHPrice()` require no privileged access. MEV/EL rewards accumulate continuously in `FeeReceiver`. The attack requires only monitoring the `FeeReceiver` balance and submitting two to three transactions. No oracle manipulation, reentrancy, or governance capture is needed. The attack is repeatable indefinitely for rewards below the `pricePercentageLimit` threshold, and unconditionally repeatable when `pricePercentageLimit == 0`.

## Recommendation
1. **Restrict `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()`** to a privileged role (e.g., `MANAGER` or `OPERATOR`) so that reward injection timing is controlled by the protocol, not arbitrary callers.
2. **Alternatively**, call `_updateRsETHPrice()` atomically inside `receiveFromRewardReceiver()` so that any deposit made before reward injection is priced *after* the reward is reflected, eliminating the timing gap.
3. **Ensure `pricePercentageLimit` is always set** to a non-zero value as defense-in-depth.

## Proof of Concept
```solidity
// Foundry fork test (pseudocode)
function testMEVFrontRun() public {
    vm.deal(address(feeReceiver), 10 ether); // simulate accumulated MEV rewards

    uint256 priceBefore = lrtOracle.rsETHPrice();

    // Step 1: attacker deposits at stale price
    vm.prank(attacker);
    depositPool.depositETH{value: 100 ether}(0, "");
    uint256 attackerRsETH = rsETH.balanceOf(attacker);

    // Step 2: attacker injects reward into TVL
    vm.prank(attacker);
    feeReceiver.sendFunds();

    // Step 3: attacker triggers price update
    vm.prank(attacker);
    lrtOracle.updateRSETHPrice();

    uint256 priceAfter = lrtOracle.rsETHPrice();
    assertGt(priceAfter, priceBefore);

    // Step 4: after withdrawal delay, attacker redeems at higher price
    uint256 attackerETHValue = attackerRsETH * priceAfter / 1e18;
    assertGt(attackerETHValue, 100 ether); // attacker captured yield they didn't earn
}
```
The assertion holds for any non-zero `FeeReceiver` balance, as long as the price increase stays within `pricePercentageLimit` (or `pricePercentageLimit == 0`).
