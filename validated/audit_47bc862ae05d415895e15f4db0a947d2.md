### Title
Stale `CommitteeAuthorization` Not Cleared on Committee Member Removal Enables Unauthorized Governance Votes — (`eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/Enact.hs`)

---

### Summary

When a committee cold key is removed from the committee via an enacted `UpdateCommittee` or `NoConfidence` governance action, the corresponding `CommitteeAuthorization` entry in `CommitteeState.csCommitteeCreds` is **not deleted**. When the cold key is subsequently re-added to the committee, the stale hot key authorization is immediately active, allowing the old hot key holder to cast votes on governance actions without the cold key holder's re-consent.

---

### Finding Description

The Conway governance system maintains two separate data structures for committee membership:

1. **`Committee.committeeMembers`** (inside `EnactState.ensCommittee`) — the authoritative set of current committee members and their term expiry epochs.
2. **`CommitteeState.csCommitteeCreds`** (inside `CertState.vsCommitteeState`) — a map from cold credential to `CommitteeAuthorization` (`CommitteeHotCredential hotKey` or `CommitteeMemberResigned anchor`).

The `ENACT` rule handles `UpdateCommittee` and `NoConfidence` governance actions:

```haskell
-- Enact.hs lines 104-111
NoConfidence _ ->
  st
    & ensCommitteeL .~ SNothing          -- dissolves the committee
    & ensPrevCommitteeL .~ SJust (GovPurposeId govActionId)
UpdateCommittee _ membersToRemove membersToAdd newThreshold -> do
  st
    & ensCommitteeL %~ SJust . updatedCommittee membersToRemove membersToAdd newThreshold
    & ensPrevCommitteeL .~ SJust (GovPurposeId govActionId)
```

In both cases, only `EnactState.ensCommittee` is updated. `CommitteeState.csCommitteeCreds` is **never touched**.

The `GOVCERT` rule is the only place that modifies `csCommitteeCreds`, via `AuthCommitteeHotKey` and `ResignCommitteeColdKey` certificates. However, `checkAndOverwriteCommitteeMemberState` enforces that the cold key must be either a current committee member or present in a pending `UpdateCommittee` proposal:

```haskell
-- GovCert.hs lines 197-205
let isCurrentMember =
      strictMaybe False (Map.member coldCred . committeeMembers) cgceCurrentCommittee
    isPotentialFutureMember =
      any committeeUpdateContainsColdCred cgceCommitteeProposals
isCurrentMember || isPotentialFutureMember ?! (injectFailure . ConwayCommitteeIsUnknown) coldCred
```

After a cold key is removed from the committee (and is not in any pending proposal), it **cannot** submit `AuthCommitteeHotKey` or `ResignCommitteeColdKey`. The cold key holder loses the ability to proactively clear or change their hot key authorization.

The ratification logic in `committeeAcceptedRatio` iterates over `Committee.committeeMembers` and looks up each cold key in `csCommitteeCreds`:

```haskell
-- Ratify.hs lines 154-162
case Map.lookup member (csCommitteeCreds committeeState) of
  Nothing -> (yes, tot)                          -- abstain
  Just (CommitteeMemberResigned _) -> (yes, tot) -- abstain
  Just (CommitteeHotCredential hotKey) ->
    case Map.lookup hotKey votes of
      Nothing    -> (yes, tot + 1)               -- no
      Just VoteYes -> (yes + 1, tot + 1)         -- yes
```

When the cold key is re-added to the committee, the stale `CommitteeHotCredential hotKey` entry in `csCommitteeCreds` is immediately consulted by ratification. The old hot key holder can vote on governance actions on behalf of the cold key holder without any re-authorization.

---

### Impact Explanation

An attacker who controls a stale hot key (previously authorized by a committee cold key holder) can vote on governance actions — including `TreasuryWithdrawals`, `ParameterChange`, `HardForkInitiation`, `NewConstitution`, and `UpdateCommittee` — on behalf of the cold key holder, without the cold key holder's current consent. If the attacker's vote is the deciding vote (e.g., the committee threshold is 1/2 and the attacker controls one of two members), an unauthorized governance action can be enacted.

This matches: **Critical. Unauthorized governance, treasury, protocol-parameter, committee, constitution, or hard-fork action is enacted.**

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The scenario is realistic in the context of `NoConfidence` actions, which dissolve the entire committee. After `NoConfidence`:
- All `CommitteeAuthorization` entries persist in `csCommitteeCreds`.
- No committee member can clear their hot key authorization (no current committee, no pending proposal).
- When a new committee is elected with any of the same cold keys, all stale hot key authorizations are immediately active.

The attacker entry path is: submit a `VotingProcedure` transaction using the stale hot key credential immediately after the `UpdateCommittee` enactment epoch boundary, before the cold key holder can submit a new `AuthCommitteeHotKey` certificate.

---

### Recommendation

When `UpdateCommittee` or `NoConfidence` is enacted, the `CommitteeAuthorization` entries for removed cold keys should be deleted from `CommitteeState.csCommitteeCreds`. Since the `ENACT` rule's state is `EnactState` (not `CertState`), this cleanup must be performed in the higher-level rule that calls `ENACT` and has access to `CertState` (the `EPOCH`/`NEWEPOCH` rule). Concretely, after enacting `UpdateCommittee`, delete `csCommitteeCreds` entries for all keys in `membersToRemove`; after enacting `NoConfidence`, delete all entries in `csCommitteeCreds`.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Committee member cold key `C1` authorizes hot key `H1` (controlled by attacker `A`) via `AuthCommitteeHotKeyTxCert`. `csCommitteeCreds[C1] = CommitteeHotCredential H1`.
2. A `NoConfidence` governance action is ratified and enacted. `ensCommitteeL .~ SNothing`. `csCommitteeCreds[C1]` is **not deleted**.
3. `C1` attempts to submit `ResignCommitteeColdTxCert` to clear `H1` — this fails with `ConwayCommitteeIsUnknown C1` because `C1` is no longer a current member and no pending `UpdateCommittee` proposal includes `C1`.
4. A new `UpdateCommittee` governance action is enacted, re-adding `C1` to the committee. `csCommitteeCreds[C1]` still holds `CommitteeHotCredential H1`.
5. Attacker `A` immediately submits a `VotingProcedure` using `H1` to vote `VoteYes` on a `TreasuryWithdrawals` governance action. `committeeAcceptedRatio` finds `CommitteeHotCredential H1` for `C1` and counts the vote.
6. The treasury withdrawal is enacted with `C1`'s unauthorized vote contributing to the ratification threshold.

committeeAcceptedRatio members votes committeeState currentEpoch =
  yesVotes %? totalExcludingAbstain
  where
    accumVotes ::
      (Integer, Integer) ->
      Credential ColdCommitteeRole ->
      EpochNo ->
      (Integer, Integer)
    accumVotes (!yes, !tot) member expiry
      | currentEpoch > expiry = (yes, tot) -- member is expired, vote "abstain" (don't count it)
      | otherwise =
          case Map.lookup member (csCommitteeCreds committeeState) of
            Nothing -> (yes, tot) -- member is not registered, vote "abstain"
            Just (CommitteeMemberResigned _) -> (yes, tot) -- member has resigned, vote "abstain"
            Just (CommitteeHotCredential hotKey) ->
              case Map.lookup hotKey votes of
                Nothing -> (yes, tot + 1) -- member hasn't voted, vote "no"
                Just Abstain -> (yes, tot) -- member voted "abstain"
                Just VoteNo -> (yes, tot + 1) -- member voted "no"
                Just VoteYes -> (yes + 1, tot + 1) -- member voted "yes"
    (yesVotes, totalExcludingAbstain) = Map.foldlWithKey' accumVotes (0, 0) members

data CommitteeAuthorization
  = -- | Member authorized with a Hot credential acting on behalf of their Cold credential
    CommitteeHotCredential !(Credential HotCommitteeRole)
  | -- | Member resigned with a potential explanation in Anchor
    CommitteeMemberResigned !(StrictMaybe Anchor)
  deriving (Eq, Ord, Show, Generic)

instance NoThunks CommitteeAuthorization

instance NFData CommitteeAuthorization

instance ToJSON CommitteeAuthorization

instance EncCBOR CommitteeAuthorization where
  encCBOR =
    encode . \case
      CommitteeHotCredential cred -> Sum CommitteeHotCredential 0 !> To cred
      CommitteeMemberResigned anchor -> Sum CommitteeMemberResigned 1 !> To anchor

instance DecCBOR CommitteeAuthorization where
  decCBOR =
    decode $ Summands "CommitteeAuthorization" $ \case
      0 -> SumD CommitteeHotCredential <! From
      1 -> SumD CommitteeMemberResigned <! From
      k -> Invalid k

newtype CommitteeState era = CommitteeState
  { csCommitteeCreds :: Map (Credential ColdCommitteeRole) CommitteeAuthorization
  }
  deriving (Eq, Ord, Show, Generic, EncCBOR, NFData, Default, NoThunks)
