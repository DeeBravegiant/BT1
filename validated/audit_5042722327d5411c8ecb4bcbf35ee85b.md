### Title
Meta Transaction Deposit Refund Race Condition Allows Sender to Steal Relayer's Attached Deposit - (File: runtime/runtime/src/actions.rs)

### Summary
In NEAR's meta transaction (NEP-366) implementation, when a `DelegateAction`'s inner receipt fails on the receiver's shard, the attached NEAR deposit is refunded to the sender (`predecessor_id` = Alice), not the relayer who actually paid it. An unprivileged user can exploit this race condition: they submit a meta transaction with a large deposit that appears valid at check time, then drain their fungible token balance between the relayer's off-chain validation and on-chain execution, causing the inner receipt to fail and the relayer's deposit to be transferred to the attacker.

### Finding Description
In `apply_delegate_action`, when a `DelegateAction` is processed on the sender's shard, a new receipt is created with `predecessor_id: sender_id.clone()` (Alice). The relayer (`signer_id`) prepays the attached NEAR deposit for the inner actions. When this inner receipt subsequently fails on the receiver's shard, the deposit refund logic calls `receipt.balance_refund_receiver()`, which returns the predecessor (Alice), not the signer/relayer. The race condition window is the block production time between the relayer's off-chain validation check and the on-chain execution of the inner receipt. During this window, Alice can drain her fungible token balance (e.g., by submitting a direct FT transfer), causing the inner function call to fail. The NEAR deposit paid by the relayer is then refunded to Alice.

The relayer cannot close this window: even if it validates Alice's FT balance immediately before submitting, Alice can submit a competing transaction in the same or next block that drains her FT balance before the meta transaction's inner receipt executes. [5](#0-4) 

### Impact Explanation
The relayer loses NEAR tokens equal to the attached deposit in the meta transaction. Alice gains those tokens. The corrupted protocol value is the relayer's NEAR account balance (decreases by the deposit amount) and Alice's NEAR account balance (increases by the same amount). This is a direct, irreversible financial loss for the relayer with no recourse.

### Likelihood Explanation
Low. Requires Alice to:
1. Craft a `SignedDelegateAction` with a large NEAR deposit attached to a function call that appears valid at check time (e.g., an FT transfer she has sufficient balance for at validation time).
2. Submit a competing FT drain transaction in the same or next block, before the meta transaction's inner receipt executes on the receiver's shard.

The race condition window spans at least one block (~1 second), which is sufficient for Alice to submit a competing transaction. The attack is repeatable and can be automated.

### Recommendation
1. When a meta transaction's inner receipt fails, refund the deposit to the signer (relayer), not the predecessor (sender). This requires tracking the original payer through the receipt chain.
2. Alternatively, allow the relayer to specify a `refund_to` field in the `DelegateAction` that overrides the default predecessor-based refund recipient.
3. Alternatively, implement a two-phase commit: the relayer holds the deposit in escrow until the inner receipt succeeds, only releasing it to the receiver on success and returning it to the relayer on failure.

### Proof of Concept
1. Alice creates a `SignedDelegateAction` with 100 NEAR deposit attached to `ft_transfer("john", 10)` on a FT contract. Alice currently holds 10 FT.
2. Alice submits it to the relayer.
3. The relayer validates Alice's FT balance (10 FT ≥ 10 FT required) and submits the meta transaction, paying 100 NEAR deposit from its own account.
4. Alice immediately submits a direct `ft_transfer` to drain her FT balance to 0 (this lands in the same or next block).
5. The meta transaction executes: `apply_delegate_action` creates a new receipt with `predecessor_id = Alice` and sends it to the FT contract shard.
6. The FT contract's `ft_transfer` fails because Alice now has 0 FT.
7. The deposit refund logic at `runtime/runtime/src/lib.rs:1270-1273` sends 100 NEAR to `receipt.balance_refund_receiver()` = Alice (predecessor).
8. Alice has stolen 100 NEAR from the relayer. The relayer also lost gas costs with no compensation.

The root cause is the combination of `predecessor_id: sender_id.clone()` at `runtime/runtime/src/actions.rs:457` (setting Alice as predecessor of the inner receipt) and the unconditional predecessor-based deposit refund at `runtime/runtime/src/lib.rs:1270-1273`. [6](#0-5) [7](#0-6)
