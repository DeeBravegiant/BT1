## Title
Unbounded O(assets Ă— NDCs) Gas in Permissionless `updateRSETHPrice()` Enables Block Stuffing â€” (`contracts/LRTOracle.sol`)

## Summary

`updateRSETHPrice()` is a permissionless `public` function with no cooldown or rate-limiting guard. Its internal call chain traverses a nested loop whose gas cost scales as O(supported assets Ă— NDCs), with no on-chain cap on either dimension. An unprivileged attacker can invoke it repeatedly within a single block to consume the block gas limit, crowding out all other transactions.

## Finding Description

`updateRSETHPrice()` (LRTOracle.sol L87â€“89) calls `_updateRsETHPrice()` â†’ `_getTotalEthInProtocol()` (L331â€“349). `_getTotalEthInProtocol()` iterates over every entry in `lrtConfig.getSupportedAssetList()`. Total external calls per invocation: `N_assets Ă— (1 oracle + 3 Ă— N_NDCs)`.

There is no cap on `supportedAssetList` length (LRTConfig.sol L106â€“118) and `maxNodeDelegatorLimit` defaults to 10 and is freely adjustable upward by admin (LRTDepositPool.sol L49, L290â€“296). No cooldown, block-number guard, or per-block invocation limit exists anywhere in `LRTOracle.sol`.

**Repeated-call bypass:** When TVL is flat between calls, `protocolFeeInETH == 0` (LRTOracle.sol L243â€“247), so the else-branch calls `_checkAndUpdateDailyFeeMintLimit(0)` (L309â€“311). The price-threshold guard at L252â€“267 also does not trigger because `newRsETHPrice == rsETHPrice == highestRsethPrice` when TVL is unchanged. Every repeated call within the same block succeeds.

## Impact Explanation

**Low â€” Block stuffing.** At realistic protocol scale (10 assets, 10 NDCs), a single `updateRSETHPrice()` invocation executes 310+ external calls. At â‰Ą2,100 gas per cold SLOAD/call, plus EigenLayer strategy reads and Chainlink oracle reads, each invocation costs on the order of 1â€“3 M gas. An attacker can pack ~10â€“30 such calls into a single 30 M gas Ethereum block, crowding out all other protocol transactions (deposits, withdrawals, claims) for that block. The attacker bears the gas cost but requires no privilege.

## Likelihood Explanation

**Low-to-Medium.** The preconditions â€” multiple supported assets and NDCs â€” arise naturally from protocol growth, not from attacker-controlled state. `addNewSupportedAsset` requires `TIME_LOCK_ROLE` and `addNodeDelegatorContractToQueue` requires `onlyLRTAdmin`; both are expected to be exercised legitimately over time. Once those preconditions exist, any EOA can trigger the attack at any time with no further setup. The only cost is gas.

## Recommendation

1. **Add a per-block or per-N-seconds cooldown** to `updateRSETHPrice()`: store `lastUpdateBlock` or `lastUpdateTimestamp` and revert if called again within the same block (or within a minimum interval).
2. **Enforce a hard cap** on `supportedAssetList.length` and `maxNodeDelegatorLimit` such that the worst-case gas of one `updateRSETHPrice()` call is provably below a safe fraction of the block gas limit.
3. **Cache the TVL off-chain** (keeper/bot pattern): store the computed total ETH value on-chain and have `updateRSETHPrice()` read the cached value, restricting the expensive recomputation to a privileged keeper role.
