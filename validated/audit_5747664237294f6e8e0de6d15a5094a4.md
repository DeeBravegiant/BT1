### Title
Silent Arithmetic Overflow in `denormalize_amount` Corrupts Bridge Transfer Amounts — (`near/omni-bridge/src/lib.rs`)

### Summary

The `denormalize_amount` helper in the NEAR bridge contract performs an unchecked `u128` multiplication when converting a token amount from its normalized (capped-18-decimal) representation back to its original-decimal representation. For tokens whose `origin_decimals` significantly exceeds the normalized `decimals`, a user-supplied amount from an EVM `InitTransfer` event can cause the multiplication to overflow `u128`, either silently wrapping (if `overflow-checks` is off) or panicking (if `overflow-checks` is on). Either outcome breaks the backing guarantee: the user's EVM tokens are already locked in the EVM bridge, but the NEAR side either records a wildly incorrect amount or permanently fails to process the transfer.

---

### Finding Description

`denormalize_amount` is defined as:

```rust
fn denormalize_amount(amount: u128, decimals: Decimals) -> u128 {
    let diff_decimals: u32 = (decimals.origin_decimals - decimals.decimals).into();
    amount * (10_u128.pow(diff_decimals))   // ← unchecked multiplication
}
``` [1](#0-0) 

It is called unconditionally inside `fin_transfer_callback` with the raw amount decoded from the EVM `InitTransfer` proof:

```rust
amount: Self::denormalize_amount(init_transfer.amount.0, decimals).into(),
``` [2](#0-1) 

The `Decimals` struct stores two fields:

- `decimals` — the normalized (capped) representation used on NEAR (≤ 18)
- `origin_decimals` — the original token decimals on the source chain (e.g. 30) [3](#0-2) 

When `origin_decimals = 30` and `decimals = 18`, `diff_decimals = 12` and the function computes `amount × 10¹²`. The EVM `InitTransfer` event carries a `uint128 amount` (user-controlled, up to `2¹²⁸ − 1`). Any value above `u128::MAX / 10¹²  ≈ 3.4 × 10²⁶` (i.e. roughly 340 million tokens expressed in 18-decimal units) causes the multiplication to exceed `u128::MAX`.

The EVM-side `initTransfer` accepts `uint128 amount` with no upper-bound check relative to what NEAR can safely denormalize: [4](#0-3) 

The EVM event definition confirms the amount field is `uint128` with no normalization applied before emission: [5](#0-4) 

---

### Impact Explanation

**If `overflow-checks = true` (standard for NEAR release builds):** the multiplication panics, the `fin_transfer_callback` transaction is aborted, and the transfer is never recorded on NEAR. The user's EVM tokens are already held by the EVM bridge contract with no automatic refund path. The transfer is permanently unclaimable — an irreversible fund lock.

**If `overflow-checks = false`:** the result wraps silently to a small value. The NEAR bridge records a drastically understated amount, mints or releases far fewer tokens to the recipient than were locked on EVM, and the excess backing is permanently stranded. This breaks the 1:1 backing guarantee.

Both outcomes fall within the allowed impact scope:
- *Irreversible fund lock / permanently unclaimable user value* (panic path)
- *Balance-accounting divergence that breaks backing guarantees* (wrap path)

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- Any unprivileged user can call `initTransfer` on the EVM bridge with an arbitrary `uint128 amount`.
- The EVM contract performs no check that the amount is safe to denormalize on NEAR.
- Tokens with `origin_decimals > 18` are explicitly supported (the `_normalizeDecimals` cap in Starknet and the `origin_decimals` field in `DeployToken` events confirm this design).
- The overflow threshold (~340 million tokens in 18-decimal units for a 30-decimal token) is within the circulating supply of many real-world tokens.
- No privileged role, leaked key, or external oracle compromise is required. [6](#0-5) 

---

### Recommendation

Replace the bare multiplication in `denormalize_amount` with a checked variant and propagate the error:

```rust
fn denormalize_amount(amount: u128, decimals: Decimals) -> Result<u128, BridgeError> {
    let diff_decimals: u32 = (decimals.origin_decimals - decimals.decimals).into();
    let multiplier = 10_u128.pow(diff_decimals);
    amount.checked_mul(multiplier).ok_or(BridgeError::AmountOverflow)
}
```

All call sites (`fin_transfer_callback`, `fast_fin_transfer`, `denormalize_fee`) must propagate or panic-on-error explicitly, so that an oversized EVM amount is rejected with a clear error *before* the user's tokens are locked, rather than silently corrupting accounting or trapping mid-callback.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Deploy or register a token on EVM with `originDecimals = 30`, normalized to `decimals = 18` on NEAR.
2. Call `initTransfer` on the EVM bridge with `amount = 4 × 10²⁶` (≈ 400 million tokens in 18-decimal units, well within `uint128`).
3. Submit the resulting proof to the NEAR bridge's `fin_transfer` entry point.
4. `fin_transfer_callback` calls `denormalize_amount(4 × 10²⁶, {decimals: 18, origin_decimals: 30})`.
5. The computation is `4 × 10²⁶ × 10¹² = 4 × 10³⁸ > u128::MAX (≈ 3.4 × 10³⁸)`.
6. With `overflow-checks = true`: the callback panics; the EVM tokens remain locked with no NEAR-side record.
7. With `overflow-checks = false`: the result wraps to `≈ 1.9 × 10²⁰`, and the recipient receives only a tiny fraction of the bridged value.
