### Title
Stale `CommitteeMemberResigned` State Permanently Blocks Re-Elected Committee Member from Authorizing Hot Key — (`File: eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/GovCert.hs`)

### Summary

When a Constitutional Committee member resigns via `ConwayResignCommitteeColdKey`, the ledger records `CommitteeMemberResigned` in `vsCommitteeState`. If the same cold credential is subsequently re-elected through an enacted `UpdateCommittee` governance action, the `CommitteeMemberResigned` entry is never cleared. The `ConwayCommitteeHasPreviouslyResigned` predicate check in `checkAndOverwriteCommitteeMemberState` then permanently blocks the re-elected member from authorizing any hot key, making them unable to vote on governance actions for the remainder of their new term.

### Finding Description

In `GovCert.hs`, the function `checkAndOverwriteCommitteeMemberState` is called for both `ConwayAuthCommitteeHotKey` and `ConwayResignCommitteeColdKey` certificates: [1](#0-0) 

The check at lines 192–196 looks up the cold credential in `csCommitteeCreds`. If it finds `CommitteeMemberResigned`, it immediately fails with `ConwayCommitteeHasPreviouslyResigned` — **before** the `isCurrentMember || isPotentialFutureMember` check at line 205 is ever evaluated. [2](#0-1) 

The `CommitteeMemberResigned` entry is written to `vsCommitteeState` on resignation: [3](#0-2) 

The `VState` holding this map is: [4](#0-3) 

The ENACT rule for `UpdateCommittee` re-adds the cold credential to `committeeMembers` (the elected set), but does **not** clear the stale `CommitteeMemberResigned` entry from `vsCommitteeState`. This is confirmed by the query-spec integration test, which shows a resigned member who receives a `TermAdjusted` re-election still carries `MemberResigned` status after the epoch boundary: [5](#0-4) 

The ratification logic in `committeeAcceptedRatio` treats a resigned member as an abstain vote (excluded from both numerator and denominator): [6](#0-5) 

Because the re-elected member counts toward `ppCommitteeMinSizeL` (they are in `committeeMembers`) but cannot vote (they cannot authorize a hot key), the effective voting pool is silently reduced below what the governance threshold was designed to require.

### Impact Explanation

A re-elected committee member who previously resigned is permanently unable to authorize a hot key for their new term. They count toward the minimum committee size check (so the committee appears quorate) but contribute only an implicit abstain to every vote. This silently lowers the effective denominator in `committeeAcceptedRatio`, allowing governance actions — including `TreasuryWithdrawals`, `ParameterChange`, `HardForkInitiation`, `NewConstitution`, and `UpdateCommittee` — to be ratified with fewer real yes-votes than the configured threshold was designed to require. This constitutes an unauthorized governance action being enacted outside design parameters.

**Applicable impact**: *Critical — Unauthorized governance, treasury, protocol-parameter, committee, constitution, or hard-fork action is enacted.*

### Likelihood Explanation

The scenario requires: (1) a committee member submits `ConwayResignCommitteeColdKey` (unprivileged, routine); (2) a subsequent `UpdateCommittee` governance action re-adds the same cold credential and is enacted (requires governance majority, but is a legitimate governance operation, not a malicious one); (3) the re-elected member submits `ConwayAuthCommitteeHotKey` and is blocked. Steps 1 and 3 are unprivileged transactions. Step 2 is a normal governance process. The bug is in the ledger rule that fails to clear stale state on re-election.

### Recommendation

In the ENACT rule for `UpdateCommittee`, when a cold credential is added to the new committee, remove any existing `CommitteeMemberResigned` entry for that credential from `vsCommitteeState`. Concretely, after enacting an `UpdateCommittee` action, apply:

```haskell
& certVStateL . vsCommitteeStateL . csCommitteeCredsL
    %~ \creds -> foldr Map.delete creds (Map.keys membersToAdd)
```

This mirrors the fix described in the external report: check whether the referenced entity has been "destroyed" (resigned) and, if it has been legitimately re-created (re-elected), clear the stale entry before applying the existence guard.

### Proof of Concept

1. Register a committee member cold key `coldC`.
2. Submit and enact an `UpdateCommittee` proposal electing `coldC`.
3. Register a hot key for `coldC` via `ConwayAuthCommitteeHotKey` — succeeds.
4. Submit `ConwayResignCommitteeColdKey coldC` — sets `CommitteeMemberResigned coldC` in `vsCommitteeState`.
5. Submit and enact a second `UpdateCommittee` proposal re-electing `coldC` with a new term.
6. Attempt `ConwayAuthCommitteeHotKey coldC newHotKey` — **fails** with `ConwayCommitteeHasPreviouslyResigned coldC`.
7. `coldC` is now counted in `committeeMembers` (satisfying `ppCommitteeMinSizeL`) but permanently abstains, silently reducing the effective voting denominator for all subsequent governance ratification. [1](#0-0) [7](#0-6)

committeeAcceptedRatio members votes committeeState currentEpoch =
  yesVotes %? totalExcludingAbstain
  where
    accumVotes ::
      (Integer, Integer) ->
      Credential ColdCommitteeRole ->
      EpochNo ->
      (Integer, Integer)
    accumVotes (!yes, !tot) member expiry
      | currentEpoch > expiry = (yes, tot) -- member is expired, vote "abstain" (don't count it)
      | otherwise =
          case Map.lookup member (csCommitteeCreds committeeState) of
            Nothing -> (yes, tot) -- member is not registered, vote "abstain"
            Just (CommitteeMemberResigned _) -> (yes, tot) -- member has resigned, vote "abstain"
            Just (CommitteeHotCredential hotKey) ->
              case Map.lookup hotKey votes of
                Nothing -> (yes, tot + 1) -- member hasn't voted, vote "no"
                Just Abstain -> (yes, tot) -- member voted "abstain"
                Just VoteNo -> (yes, tot + 1) -- member voted "no"
                Just VoteYes -> (yes + 1, tot + 1) -- member voted "yes"
    (yesVotes, totalExcludingAbstain) = Map.foldlWithKey' accumVotes (0, 0) members
