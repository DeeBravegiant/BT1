### Title
Last-Minute DRep Unregistration Removes Votes and Manipulates Governance Ratification Outcome - (File: `eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/Gov.hs`)

### Summary

The Conway governance system allows a DRep to unregister (`ConwayUnRegDRep`) at any time, including in the last block of an epoch immediately before the `RATIFY` rule runs. Unregistration atomically removes the DRep's votes from every in-flight governance action and excludes their delegated stake from the active-voting-stake denominator. An attacker who controls a DRep with sufficient stake can exploit this timing to flip a ratification outcome — either forcing a previously-failing governance action to pass (by removing a large "No" vote) or preventing a previously-passing action from being enacted (by removing a large "Yes" vote).

### Finding Description

**Step 1 — Vote removal on unregistration (`Gov.hs`)**

When the `GOV` rule processes a transaction that contains an `UnRegDRepTxCert`, it collects all unregistering DRep credentials and strips their votes from every proposal in the current governance state: [1](#0-0) 

There is no guard on *when* during the epoch this certificate may be submitted.

**Step 2 — Unregistered DRep excluded from ratification denominator (`Ratify.hs`)**

`dRepAcceptedRatio` iterates over `reDRepDistr` (the stake-distribution snapshot). For each `DRepCredential cred`, it looks up `cred` in `reDRepState`. If the DRep is absent (because they just unregistered), their entire delegated stake is excluded from both numerator and denominator: [2](#0-1) 

**Step 3 — No timing restriction in `GovCert.hs`**

The `ConwayUnRegDRep` handler imposes only two checks: the DRep must exist and the refund must match the deposit. There is no epoch-boundary buffer, no lock-up period, and no restriction preventing unregistration in the final slot of an epoch: [3](#0-2) 

**Concrete attack (making a failing action pass)**

| Actor | Stake share | Vote |
|---|---|---|
| DRep A (attacker) | 60 % | No |
| DRep B | 40 % | Yes |

Threshold = 50 %. Before unregistration: `40 / 100 = 40 %` → **fails**.

Attacker submits `UnRegDRepTxCert` for DRep A in the last block of the epoch:
- DRep A's "No" vote is removed from `gasDRepVotes`.
- At ratification, DRep A is absent from `reDRepState`; their 60 % stake is excluded from the denominator.
- Effective ratio: `40 / 40 = 100 %` → **passes**.

The treasury withdrawal (or hard-fork, or protocol-parameter change) is enacted without the required threshold of active DRep support.

### Impact Explanation

A DRep controlling enough stake to affect a close vote can unilaterally flip the ratification outcome of any governance action — including `TreasuryWithdrawals`, `ParameterChange`, `HardForkInitiation`, `UpdateCommittee`, and `NewConstitution`. In the "make it pass" direction this constitutes an **unauthorized governance, treasury, protocol-parameter, committee, constitution, or hard-fork action being enacted** (Critical). In the "make it fail" direction it permanently blocks a legitimately-ratified action for the remainder of its lifetime, potentially freezing governance progress (High).

### Likelihood Explanation

The entry path is a standard, unprivileged transaction containing only an `UnRegDRepTxCert`. The attacker must:
1. Control a DRep with enough delegated stake to affect the vote ratio.
2. Be willing to forfeit the DRep deposit (or recoup it via the benefit of the manipulated outcome).
3. Submit the transaction before the epoch boundary.

No privileged access, no key compromise, and no consensus-level attack is required. The behavior is already tested and confirmed in the existing test suite: [4](#0-3) 

### Recommendation

Introduce a buffer period (analogous to `MIN_LENGTH_BID_EXPIRY` in the external report) during which DRep unregistration is prohibited. Concretely, reject `ConwayUnRegDRep` certificates when the current epoch is the final epoch of any in-flight governance action's lifetime, or more conservatively, disallow unregistration in the last N slots/blocks of every epoch. Alternatively, preserve the DRep's votes and stake contribution for the current epoch's ratification even after unregistration is submitted, applying the removal only from the following epoch.

### Proof of Concept

1. Register DRep A with 60 % of total delegated stake; register DRep B with 40 %.
2. Submit a `TreasuryWithdrawals` governance action with a 50 % DRep threshold.
3. DRep A submits `VoteNo`; DRep B submits `VoteYes`. `isDRepAccepted` returns `False`.
4. In the last block of the epoch, DRep A submits a transaction containing only `UnRegDRepTxCert drepCredA deposit`.
5. Observe via `getGovActionState` that `gasDRepVotes` no longer contains DRep A's entry (vote removed by `cleanupProposalVotes`).
6. Pass the epoch. `RATIFY` runs with DRep A absent from `reDRepState`; their 60 % stake is excluded from the denominator.
7. `isDRepAccepted` now returns `True` and the treasury withdrawal is enacted, confirmed by `getTreasury` returning `zero`. [5](#0-4) [6](#0-5)

  forall era.
  ( Embed (EraRule "ENACT" era) (RATIFY era)
  , State (EraRule "ENACT" era) ~ EnactState era
  , Environment (EraRule "ENACT" era) ~ ()
  , Signal (EraRule "ENACT" era) ~ EnactSignal era
  , ConwayEraPParams era
  , ConwayEraAccounts era
  ) =>
  TransitionRule (RATIFY era)
ratifyTransition = do
  TRC
    ( env@RatifyEnv {reCurrentEpoch}
      , st@( RatifyState
               rsEnactState@EnactState
                 { ensCurPParams
                 , ensTreasury
                 , ensPrevGovActionIds
                 }
               _rsEnacted
               _rsExpired
               rsDelayed
             )
      , RatifySignal rsig
      ) <-
    judgmentContext
  case rsig of
    gas@GovActionState {gasId, gasExpiresAfter} SSeq.:<| sigs -> do
      let govAction = gasAction gas
      if prevActionAsExpected gas ensPrevGovActionIds
        && validCommitteeTerm govAction ensCurPParams reCurrentEpoch
        && not rsDelayed
        && withdrawalCanWithdraw govAction ensTreasury
        && acceptedByEveryone env st gas
        then do
          newEnactState <-
            trans @(EraRule "ENACT" era) $
              TRC ((), rsEnactState, EnactSignal gasId govAction)
          let
            st' =
              st
                & rsEnactStateL .~ newEnactState
                & rsDelayedL .~ delayingAction govAction
                & rsEnactedL %~ (Seq.:|> gas)
          trans @(RATIFY era) $ TRC (env, st', RatifySignal sigs)
        else do
          -- This action hasn't been ratified yet. Process the remaining actions.
          st' <- trans @(RATIFY era) $ TRC (env, st, RatifySignal sigs)
          -- Finally, filter out actions that have expired.
          if gasExpiresAfter < reCurrentEpoch
            then pure $ st' & rsExpiredL %~ Set.insert gasId
            else pure st'
    SSeq.Empty -> pure $ st & rsEnactStateL . ensTreasuryL .~ Coin 0
