Audit Report

## Title
Premature `totalKernelStaked` Decrement in `initiateWithdrawal` Inflates Rewards for Remaining Stakers at Withdrawing Users' Expense - (File: contracts/KERNEL/KernelDepositPool.sol)

## Summary
`KernelDepositPool.initiateWithdrawal` immediately decrements both `balanceOf[msg.sender]` and `totalKernelStaked` at the moment of call, even though the KERNEL tokens remain locked in the contract for the full `withdrawalDelay` (up to 30 days). Because `rewardPerToken()` divides by `totalKernelStaked` and `earned()` multiplies by `balanceOf`, the withdrawing user earns zero rewards during the entire lock period while remaining stakers receive a proportionally inflated reward rate — a direct, unconditional redistribution of the withdrawing user's yield to others.

## Finding Description
In `initiateWithdrawal`, the accounting variables are decremented before any token transfer occurs:

```solidity
// lines 325-326
balanceOf[msg.sender] -= _amount;
totalKernelStaked -= _amount;
``` [1](#0-0) 

The tokens are not transferred out at this point — `claimWithdrawal` (lines 344–379) is the only place where `kernelToken.safeTransfer` is called, and it is gated by `block.timestamp >= withdrawal.unlockTime`. [2](#0-1) 

After the decrement, `rewardPerToken()` computes rewards over the now-reduced `totalKernelStaked`:

```solidity
// lines 412-413
return rewardPerTokenStored + (rewardRate * (lastTimeRewardApplicable() - updatedAt) * DECIMAL_PRECISION)
    / totalKernelStaked;
``` [3](#0-2) 

And `earned()` multiplies by the now-zeroed `balanceOf[_account]`:

```solidity
// lines 421-423
return (balanceOf[_account] * (rewardPerToken() - userRewardPerTokenPaid[_account]) / DECIMAL_PRECISION)
    + rewards[_account];
``` [4](#0-3) 

The `updateReward` modifier snapshots accrued rewards up to the moment of `initiateWithdrawal`, but from that point forward the withdrawing user's `balanceOf` is zero, so they accrue nothing for the entire lock window. The fixed `rewardRate` is now divided among a smaller `totalKernelStaked`, inflating every remaining staker's per-token reward rate for the full delay period. No existing guard prevents this: the `updateReward` modifier correctly checkpoints past rewards but cannot compensate for the future accrual loss caused by the premature decrement. [5](#0-4) 

## Impact Explanation
**High — Theft of unclaimed yield.** The withdrawing user's tokens are economically captive for up to `MAX_WITHDRAWAL_DELAY = 30 days` — they cannot be restaked, transferred, or deployed elsewhere — yet they earn zero rewards during that window. [6](#0-5) 
The forfeited yield is not burned; it is unconditionally redistributed to remaining stakers. The magnitude is `rewardRate × withdrawalDelay × (withdrawnAmount / totalKernelStaked_before)`, which is material at any meaningful stake size and a 30-day delay. This matches the allowed impact class "High — Theft of unclaimed yield."

## Likelihood Explanation
**High.** Every user who calls `initiateWithdrawal` is affected unconditionally. No special conditions, no attacker, no front-running, no admin involvement required. The withdrawal path is the normal, expected exit flow for all stakers. The mandatory lock period (`withdrawalDelay`, up to 30 days) makes the yield-loss window large and unavoidable. [7](#0-6) 

## Recommendation
Track tokens in the pending-withdrawal state separately (e.g., `totalPendingWithdrawal`) and include them in the reward denominator in `rewardPerToken()`. Maintain a per-user `pendingWithdrawalBalance` and include it in `earned()`. Decrement `totalPendingWithdrawal` only when `claimWithdrawal` actually transfers tokens out. This ensures withdrawing users continue to earn rewards proportional to their locked capital until the tokens are physically returned.

## Proof of Concept
**Setup:** Alice and Bob each stake 1000 KERNEL. `totalKernelStaked = 2000`. Admin calls `notifyRewardAmount` with `rewardRate = 1 token/second` and `duration = 30 days`.

**Step 1:** Alice calls `initiateWithdrawal(1000)`.
- `updateReward(Alice)` snapshots rewards to this point (zero, since no time has elapsed).
- `balanceOf[Alice] = 0`, `totalKernelStaked = 1000` immediately.
- Alice's 1000 KERNEL remain locked in the contract.

**Step 2:** 30 days pass. `rewardPerToken()` accumulates:
```
rewardRate * 2592000 * 1e18 / 1000  // denominator is 1000, not 2000
```
Bob's `earned()` = `1000 * (accumulated rewardPerToken) / 1e18` ≈ 2,592,000 tokens (100% of rewards).

**Step 3:** Bob calls `getReward()` and receives ~2,592,000 reward tokens.

**Step 4:** Alice calls `claimWithdrawal` and receives only her 1000 KERNEL principal — zero rewards for 30 days of locked, inaccessible capital.

**Expected:** Alice should have earned ~50% of rewards (~1,296,000 tokens) since her tokens were locked and unavailable. Instead, Bob received 100%.

A Foundry test can confirm this by: (1) staking equal amounts for two users, (2) calling `notifyRewardAmount`, (3) calling `initiateWithdrawal` for one user, (4) warping 30 days, (5) asserting `earned(alice) == 0` and `earned(bob) ≈ full reward amount`. [7](#0-6)

        });
        userWithdrawalIds[msg.sender].push(withdrawalId);

        emit WithdrawalInitiated(msg.sender, _amount, withdrawalId, unlockTime);
    }
