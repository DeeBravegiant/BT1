Audit Report

## Title
Withdrawing Users Lose All Staking Rewards During Mandatory Delay Period - (File: contracts/KERNEL/KernelDepositPool.sol)

## Summary
`initiateWithdrawal()` immediately decrements both `balanceOf[msg.sender]` and `totalKernelStaked` at the moment of initiation, while the tokens remain locked in the contract for up to `MAX_WITHDRAWAL_DELAY = 30 days`. Because `earned()` computes rewards solely from `balanceOf[_account]` and `rewardPerToken()` divides by `totalKernelStaked`, the withdrawing user accrues zero rewards on their pending tokens for the entire delay window. Those rewards are instead redistributed proportionally to all remaining stakers, constituting theft of unclaimed yield.

## Finding Description
In `initiateWithdrawal()`, after the `updateReward(msg.sender)` modifier snapshots the user's earned rewards up to that point, the function immediately reduces both accounting variables:

```solidity
balanceOf[msg.sender] -= _amount;
totalKernelStaked -= _amount;
``` [1](#0-0) 

The `earned()` function computes future reward accrual exclusively from `balanceOf[_account]`:

```solidity
return (balanceOf[_account] * (rewardPerToken() - userRewardPerTokenPaid[_account]) / DECIMAL_PRECISION)
    + rewards[_account];
``` [2](#0-1) 

And `rewardPerToken()` divides by `totalKernelStaked`:

```solidity
return rewardPerTokenStored + (rewardRate * (lastTimeRewardApplicable() - updatedAt) * DECIMAL_PRECISION)
    / totalKernelStaked;
``` [3](#0-2) 

Because `totalKernelStaked` is reduced immediately, the reward rate per token for remaining stakers increases. The withdrawing user's tokens are physically held by the contract — they cannot be used elsewhere — yet they contribute zero to that user's reward accrual and zero to the denominator. The full reward stream attributable to those tokens is silently redistributed to other stakers for the entire delay window, which can be up to:

```solidity
uint256 public constant MAX_WITHDRAWAL_DELAY = 30 days;
``` [4](#0-3) 

The root cause is that the contract was adapted from Synthetix's `StakingRewards.sol` (which has no withdrawal delay) without adjusting reward accounting to account for the delay period. [5](#0-4) 

## Impact Explanation
**High — Theft of unclaimed yield.** A user who initiates a withdrawal of `X` KERNEL tokens loses all staking rewards on `X` for the entire delay period (up to 30 days). Those rewards are not burned or returned to the protocol treasury — they are redistributed proportionally to all remaining stakers. The withdrawing user suffers a direct, quantifiable loss of yield that accrues to other participants. With a 30-day delay and a meaningful reward rate, the loss per user can be substantial.

## Likelihood Explanation
**High.** Every user who calls `initiateWithdrawal()` is unconditionally affected. This is a standard, expected user action with no special preconditions. The delay is mandatory and can be up to 30 days. No attacker coordination is required — the loss occurs automatically for any withdrawing staker.

## Recommendation
Decouple reward accrual accounting from withdrawal eligibility accounting. Options:

1. Introduce a `pendingWithdrawalBalance` mapping that tracks tokens in the delay queue, and include it in reward calculations: `balanceOf[account] + pendingWithdrawalBalance[account]`. Only remove tokens from the reward-bearing balance when `claimWithdrawal()` is called and tokens are actually transferred out.
2. Do not reduce `totalKernelStaked` or `balanceOf[msg.sender]` in `initiateWithdrawal()`. Instead, reduce them only in `claimWithdrawal()`, and track pending withdrawal amounts separately to enforce the `InsufficientStakedBalance` check.

## Proof of Concept
1. Alice stakes 1000 KERNEL. `balanceOf[Alice] = 1000`, `totalKernelStaked = 1000`.
2. Bob stakes 1000 KERNEL. `balanceOf[Bob] = 1000`, `totalKernelStaked = 2000`.
3. Admin calls `notifyRewardAmount(2000e18)` with a 30-day duration → `rewardRate = 2000e18 / 30 days`.
4. Alice calls `initiateWithdrawal(1000)`. Immediately: `balanceOf[Alice] = 0`, `totalKernelStaked = 1000`. Alice's 1000 KERNEL are locked in the contract for 30 days.
5. Over the next 30 days, `rewardPerToken()` accrues at `rewardRate / 1000` (only Bob's tokens in denominator). Bob earns the full 2000e18 reward.
6. Alice calls `claimWithdrawal()` and receives her 1000 KERNEL back, but `earned(Alice) = 0` — she received no rewards for the 30-day period despite her tokens being locked in the contract the entire time.
7. Had Alice not initiated a withdrawal, she would have earned ~1000e18 reward tokens (50% of the pool). Those rewards went entirely to Bob.
