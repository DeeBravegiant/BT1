Audit Report

## Title
Pending Withdrawal Tokens Immediately Removed from Reward Accounting, Causing Loss of Yield During Mandatory Delay - (File: contracts/KERNEL/KernelDepositPool.sol)

## Summary
In `KernelDepositPool.sol`, `initiateWithdrawal()` immediately decrements both `balanceOf[msg.sender]` and `totalKernelStaked` at the moment of initiation, even though the tokens remain locked in the contract for up to `MAX_WITHDRAWAL_DELAY = 30 days`. Since `earned()` computes rewards solely from `balanceOf[_account]` and `rewardPerToken()` divides by `totalKernelStaked`, the withdrawing user accrues zero rewards on their pending tokens for the entire delay window, while those rewards are redistributed proportionally to all remaining stakers.

## Finding Description
`initiateWithdrawal()` immediately reduces both accounting variables: [1](#0-0) 

The `updateReward(msg.sender)` modifier runs before these decrements, snapshotting rewards earned up to that point. After the modifier runs, `balanceOf[msg.sender]` is set to zero for the pending amount, so no further rewards accrue: [2](#0-1) 

`earned()` computes future rewards exclusively from `balanceOf[_account]`, which is now zero for the withdrawn amount: [3](#0-2) 

`rewardPerToken()` divides by `totalKernelStaked`, which is also immediately reduced, increasing the reward rate per token for remaining stakers: [4](#0-3) 

The tokens remain physically locked in the contract until `claimWithdrawal()` is called after the delay, but contribute nothing to the withdrawing user's reward accrual and nothing to the denominator. The full reward stream attributable to those tokens is silently redistributed to other stakers. The delay can be up to 30 days: [5](#0-4) 

No existing guard prevents this — the `updateReward` modifier only snapshots rewards earned *before* the balance reduction, not during the delay period.

## Impact Explanation
**High — Theft of unclaimed yield.** Every user who calls `initiateWithdrawal()` suffers a direct, quantifiable loss of staking rewards on their pending tokens for the entire delay period (up to 30 days). Those rewards are not burned or returned to the protocol — they are redistributed proportionally to remaining stakers. This constitutes theft of unclaimed yield, which is an explicitly listed High impact in the allowed scope.

## Likelihood Explanation
**High.** Every user who calls `initiateWithdrawal()` is unconditionally and automatically affected. No attacker coordination is required. The delay is mandatory and configurable up to 30 days. This is a standard, expected user action with no special preconditions. The loss scales linearly with the amount withdrawn, the reward rate, and the delay duration.

## Recommendation
Decouple reward-bearing balance from withdrawal eligibility. Options:
1. Introduce a `pendingWithdrawalBalance` mapping and include it in `earned()` as `balanceOf[account] + pendingWithdrawalBalance[account]`, and in `totalKernelStaked`. Only remove from both when `claimWithdrawal()` executes.
2. Do not decrement `balanceOf[msg.sender]` or `totalKernelStaked` in `initiateWithdrawal()`; instead decrement them in `claimWithdrawal()` after the delay has passed (while still preventing re-staking of pending amounts).

## Proof of Concept
1. Alice stakes 1000 KERNEL: `balanceOf[Alice] = 1000`, `totalKernelStaked = 1000`.
2. Bob stakes 1000 KERNEL: `balanceOf[Bob] = 1000`, `totalKernelStaked = 2000`.
3. Admin calls `notifyRewardAmount(2000e18)` with 30-day duration → `rewardRate = 2000e18 / 30 days`.
4. Alice calls `initiateWithdrawal(1000)`. Immediately: `balanceOf[Alice] = 0`, `totalKernelStaked = 1000`. Alice's 1000 KERNEL are locked in the contract.
5. Over 30 days, `rewardPerToken()` accrues at `rewardRate / 1000` (only Bob's tokens in denominator). Bob earns the full 2000e18 reward.
6. Alice calls `claimWithdrawal()` and receives her 1000 KERNEL back, but `earned(Alice) = 0` — she received no rewards for the 30-day period despite her tokens being locked the entire time.
7. Alice's expected share was ~1000e18 (50% of pool). Those rewards went entirely to Bob.

Foundry test plan: deploy `KernelDepositPool`, stake for Alice and Bob, call `notifyRewardAmount`, call `initiateWithdrawal` for Alice, `vm.warp(block.timestamp + 30 days)`, assert `earned(Alice) == 0` and `earned(Bob) == 2000e18`.
