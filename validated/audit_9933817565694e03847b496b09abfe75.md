Audit Report

## Title
Public `updateRSETHPrice()` Triggers Automatic Pause of `LRTWithdrawalManager`, Temporarily Freezing Already-Unlocked Withdrawals — (`contracts/LRTOracle.sol`)

## Summary
`LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()` is a public, permissionless function that, when the computed rsETH price drops more than `pricePercentageLimit` below `highestRsethPrice`, automatically calls `pause()` on the `LRTWithdrawalManager`. Because `completeWithdrawal` is gated by `whenNotPaused`, users whose rsETH has already been burned during `unlockQueue` cannot collect their LST/ETH until an admin manually calls `unpause()`, creating an unbounded temporary freeze of their funds.

## Finding Description
**Permissionless entrypoint:** `updateRSETHPrice()` at `LRTOracle.sol:87` carries no role guard — any EOA or contract can call it at any time while the oracle is unpaused.

**Auto-pause trigger:** Inside `_updateRsETHPrice()` (`LRTOracle.sol:270–282`), when `newRsETHPrice < highestRsethPrice` and `diff > pricePercentageLimit.mulWad(highestRsethPrice)`, the function calls `withdrawalManager.pause()` (which requires `PAUSER_ROLE` — a role the oracle must hold for this mechanism to function), then pauses itself and returns without updating the price.

**rsETH burned before completion:** During `unlockQueue` (`LRTWithdrawalManager.sol:305`), the contract burns rsETH from its own balance. At this point the user's rsETH is gone and their request is marked unlocked (`nextLockedNonce` advanced). The user still needs to call `completeWithdrawal` to receive their LST/ETH.

**Blocked completion:** `completeWithdrawal` (`LRTWithdrawalManager.sol:183`) carries `whenNotPaused`. A pause triggered by the oracle after `unlockQueue` but before `completeWithdrawal` blocks the user from receiving assets they are already entitled to.

**Admin-only recovery:** `unpause()` (`LRTWithdrawalManager.sol:352`) is restricted to `onlyLRTAdmin`. The freeze duration is entirely dependent on admin response time.

**Existing checks insufficient:** The `whenNotPaused` guard on `completeWithdrawal` makes no distinction between requests that are still locked (where a pause is protective) and requests that are already unlocked (where a pause is punitive to users who have already burned their rsETH).

## Impact Explanation
**Medium — Temporary freezing of funds.** Users who have completed the full withdrawal lifecycle (initiated, waited the delay, had their request unlocked by an operator, had their rsETH burned) cannot call `completeWithdrawal` to receive their LST/ETH. Their assets are held in the `LRTWithdrawalManager` contract and are inaccessible until an admin unpauses. The freeze is temporary but unbounded from the user's perspective.

## Likelihood Explanation
- `updateRSETHPrice()` is callable by any unprivileged address with no preconditions beyond the oracle being unpaused.
- EigenLayer slashing is a documented, non-negligible risk that directly reduces `getTotalAssetDeposits`, lowering `newRsETHPrice`.
- A single `pricePercentageLimit` threshold governs both upward and downward moves; a modest slashing event combined with a tight limit is sufficient to trigger the auto-pause.
- No front-running, privileged access, or victim mistake is required — any actor (including a bot or a user simply wanting to update the price) can trigger the pause the moment a qualifying price drop exists on-chain.
- The condition is repeatable: after each admin unpause, the same public call can re-trigger the pause if the price condition persists.

## Recommendation
1. **Exempt `completeWithdrawal` from the pause** for already-unlocked requests, or introduce a separate `withdrawalCompletionPaused` flag so that requests past `nextLockedNonce` can always be finalized regardless of oracle-triggered pauses.
2. Alternatively, restrict `updateRSETHPrice()` to a role (e.g., `MANAGER`) so the auto-pause cannot be triggered by an arbitrary caller. The existing `updateRSETHPriceAsManager()` already provides a privileged path; making the public variant role-gated removes the permissionless trigger.
3. Consider separating the "pause new deposits/initiations" concern from the "pause completions" concern at the contract level, so that a protective pause on new activity does not retroactively freeze already-entitled withdrawals.
