### Title
Governance Action Deposit Permanently Redirected to Treasury via Unregistered Return Address During Bootstrap Phase - (File: eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/Gov.hs)

### Summary
During the Conway bootstrap phase (protocol version 9), the `GOV` rule skips the `ProposalReturnAccountDoesNotExist` check, allowing governance proposals with unregistered return addresses to be accepted. When such proposals are later removed (expired, enacted, or displaced), `returnProposalDeposits` in the `EPOCH` rule cannot credit the deposit to the unregistered credential, classifying it as `unclaimed` and permanently routing it to the treasury rather than back to the proposer.

### Finding Description
In `conwayGovTransition`, the return-address registration check is guarded by a bootstrap-phase predicate:

```haskell
unless (hardforkConwayBootstrapPhase $ pp ^. ppProtocolVersionL) $ do
  isAccountRegistered (refundAddress ^. accountAddressCredentialL) (certDState ^. accountsL)
    ?! (injectFailure . ProposalReturnAccountDoesNotExist) refundAddress
```

`hardforkConwayBootstrapPhase` returns `True` when `pvMajor pv == natVersion @9`, so the entire block is skipped during bootstrap. [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) 

The deposit amount check (`ProposalDepositIncorrect`) is **outside** the `unless` block and still runs during bootstrap, so the deposit is always collected regardless of whether the return address is registered. [3](#0-2) 

At the epoch boundary, `returnProposalDeposits` attempts to credit each removed proposal's deposit to its `gasReturnAddr`. If the credential is not registered, the deposit falls into the `unclaimed` map:

```haskell
processProposal gas (!accounts, !unclaimed)
  | (Just _accountState, newAccounts) <- updateLookupAccountState addRefund cred accounts =
      (newAccounts, unclaimed)
  | otherwise = (accounts, Map.insert (gasId gas) (gasDeposit gas) unclaimed)
  where
    cred = gasReturnAddr gas ^. accountAddressCredentialL
``` [4](#0-3) 

The `unclaimed` map is then folded into the treasury:

```haskell
chainAccountState3 =
  chainAccountState2
    & casTreasuryL <>~ (utxoState0 ^. utxosDonationL <> fold unclaimed)
``` [5](#0-4) 

The test `depositMovesToTreasuryWhenStakingAddressUnregisters` confirms this path is reachable and results in the deposit going to the treasury. [6](#0-5) 

### Impact Explanation
A transaction sender submitting a governance proposal during bootstrap with an unregistered `pProcReturnAddr` will have their full `ppGovActionDepositL` deposit permanently redirected to the treasury. The deposit is irrecoverable by the proposer without a hard fork. This modifies deposit refunds outside design parameters: the protocol's stated intent is that deposits are returned to the proposer's chosen return address, not silently absorbed by the treasury.

### Likelihood Explanation
The bootstrap phase corresponds to Conway protocol version 9. Any unprivileged proposer who (a) accidentally supplies an unregistered account address, (b) uses a wallet that generates a fresh address without registering it, or (c) deregisters their stake credential after proposal submission but before proposal removal, will trigger this path. The deposit amount (`ppGovActionDepositL`) is a substantial protocol-parameter-controlled value, making accidental loss financially significant.

### Recommendation
Remove the `unless (hardforkConwayBootstrapPhase ...)` guard from the `ProposalReturnAccountDoesNotExist` check, or add an equivalent check that applies uniformly across all protocol versions. Proposals with unregistered return addresses should be rejected at submission time in all phases, consistent with post-bootstrap behavior.

### Proof of Concept
1. Operate at protocol version 9 (bootstrap phase).
2. Submit a `ProposalProcedure` whose `pProcReturnAddr` references a credential not present in the accounts map. The deposit check passes; the return-address check is skipped.
3. Allow the proposal to expire (or be displaced by enactment).
4. At the epoch boundary, `returnProposalDeposits` finds the credential unregistered, inserts the deposit into `unclaimed`, and `epochTransition` adds it to `casTreasury`.
5. The proposer's `ppGovActionDepositL` ADA is permanently in the treasury with no recovery path.

depositMovesToTreasuryWhenStakingAddressUnregisters ::
  ConwayEraImp era => ImpTestM era ()
depositMovesToTreasuryWhenStakingAddressUnregisters = do
  disableTreasuryExpansion
  initialTreasury <- getsNES treasuryL
  modifyPParams $ \pp ->
    pp
      & ppGovActionLifetimeL .~ EpochInterval 8
      & ppGovActionDepositL .~ Coin 100
      & ppCommitteeMaxTermLengthL .~ EpochInterval 0
  returnAddr <- registerAccountAddress
  govActionDeposit <- getsNES $ nesEsL . curPParamsEpochStateL . ppGovActionDepositL
  keyDeposit <- getsNES $ nesEsL . curPParamsEpochStateL . ppKeyDepositL
  govPolicy <- getGovPolicy
  gaid <-
    mkProposalWithAccountAddress
      ( ParameterChange
          SNothing
          (emptyPParamsUpdate & ppuGovActionDepositL .~ SJust (Coin 1000000))
          govPolicy
      )
      returnAddr
      >>= submitProposal
  expectPresentGovActionId gaid
  replicateM_ 5 passEpoch
  expectTreasury initialTreasury
  expectRegisteredAccountAddress returnAddr
  submitTx_ $
    mkBasicTx mkBasicTxBody
      & bodyTxL . certsTxBodyL
        .~ SSeq.singleton
          (UnRegDepositTxCert (returnAddr ^. accountAddressCredentialL) keyDeposit)
  expectNotRegisteredRewardAddress returnAddr
  replicateM_ 5 passEpoch
  expectMissingGovActionId gaid
  expectTreasury $ initialTreasury <> govActionDeposit
