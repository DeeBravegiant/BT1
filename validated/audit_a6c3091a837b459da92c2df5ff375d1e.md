Audit Report

## Title
stETH Transfer Rounding Causes Systematic rsETH Over-Minting on Every Deposit - (File: contracts/LRTDepositPool.sol)

## Summary

`LRTDepositPool.depositAsset` computes the rsETH mint amount from the caller-supplied `depositAmount` before executing the `safeTransferFrom`. Because stETH's share-based accounting rounds down by 1–2 wei on every transfer, the contract systematically mints rsETH against a value 1–2 wei larger than the stETH it actually receives, permanently eroding the protocol's stETH backing with each deposit.

## Finding Description

The vulnerable sequence is confirmed at lines 111–115 of `contracts/LRTDepositPool.sol`:

```solidity
// L111 – rsETH calculated from user-supplied depositAmount
uint256 rsethAmountToMint = _beforeDeposit(asset, depositAmount, minRSETHAmountExpected);

// L114 – actual stETH received = depositAmount − 1 or − 2 wei (stETH share rounding)
IERC20(asset).safeTransferFrom(msg.sender, address(this), depositAmount);

// L115 – rsETH minted against depositAmount, not actual received
_mintRsETH(rsethAmountToMint);
```

`_beforeDeposit` delegates to `getRsETHAmountToMint`, which at line 520 computes:

```solidity
rsethAmountToMint = (amount * lrtOracle.getAssetPrice(asset)) / lrtOracle.rsETHPrice();
```

`amount` here is the caller-supplied `depositAmount`, not the balance delta. stETH internally tracks balances in shares; `transferFrom(sender, recipient, amount)` converts `amount` to shares (rounding down) and back, delivering `amount − 1` or `amount − 2` wei to the recipient. The contract never measures the balance before and after the transfer, so the 1–2 wei shortfall is invisible to the minting logic. No existing check compensates for this: `minRSETHAmountExpected` is a slippage floor on the minted amount, not a guard on received collateral. stETH is a first-class supported asset confirmed by 10 references in `LRTDepositPool.sol` and the presence of `LRTConstants.ST_ETH_TOKEN`, `stakeEthForStETH`, and `LRTConverter` stETH paths throughout the codebase.

## Impact Explanation

**Low – Contract fails to deliver promised returns.**

Each stETH deposit leaves the protocol holding 1–2 wei less stETH than the rsETH it issued implies. The discrepancy is permanent and irreversible: the rsETH supply grows faster than the stETH backing. Across a large number of deposits the cumulative shortfall means the final stETH redeemers cannot receive the full stETH amount their rsETH entitles them to. No single user suffers a meaningful loss, but the protocol's stETH collateralization ratio is structurally and permanently below 1:1 for stETH deposits.

## Likelihood Explanation

**Medium.** The rounding occurs unconditionally on every stETH `transferFrom` call. Any unprivileged user calling `depositAsset` with stETH triggers the path. No special role, timing, or market condition is required. The condition is deterministic and repeatable.

## Recommendation

Measure the actual stETH received by comparing balances before and after the transfer, and use the real received amount for rsETH minting:

```solidity
uint256 balanceBefore = IERC20(asset).balanceOf(address(this));
IERC20(asset).safeTransferFrom(msg.sender, address(this), depositAmount);
uint256 actualReceived = IERC20(asset).balanceOf(address(this)) - balanceBefore;

uint256 rsethAmountToMint = _beforeDeposit(asset, actualReceived, minRSETHAmountExpected);
_mintRsETH(rsethAmountToMint);
```

This pattern is already used correctly in `contracts/KERNEL/KernelDepositPool.sol` at lines 573–577 for transfer-fee tokens.

## Proof of Concept

1. Alice calls `depositAsset(stETH, 1_000_000_000, 0, "")`.
2. `_beforeDeposit` computes `rsethAmountToMint` using `1_000_000_000` as the stETH amount (line 111).
3. `safeTransferFrom` executes; stETH share rounding delivers `999_999_999` wei to the contract (line 114).
4. `_mintRsETH` mints rsETH corresponding to `1_000_000_000` wei (line 115).
5. Protocol holds 1 wei less stETH than the rsETH it issued implies.
6. Repeated across N deposits, cumulative shortfall = N × (1–2) wei of stETH, permanently unrecoverable.

A Foundry fork test can confirm this by forking mainnet, calling `depositAsset` with a real stETH holder, and asserting `IERC20(stETH).balanceOf(depositPool) == depositAmount` — the assertion will fail by 1–2 wei. [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1)
