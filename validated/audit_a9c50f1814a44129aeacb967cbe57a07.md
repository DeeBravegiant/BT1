Audit Report

## Title
Time-Unaware `pricePercentageLimit` Guard Enables DoS of Public Price Update and Stale-Price Dilution of Existing rsETH Holders - (File: contracts/LRTOracle.sol)

## Summary
`LRTOracle._updateRsETHPrice()` enforces a per-call percentage cap on price increases that is compared against the raw cumulative delta from `highestRsethPrice` with no elapsed-time scaling. If the keeper is inactive long enough for legitimate staking rewards to push the new price above the cap, every non-manager call to `updateRSETHPrice()` reverts. While the oracle is stalled, `LRTDepositPool` continues accepting deposits using the stale (lower) `rsETHPrice`, minting excess rsETH to new depositors and diluting the accumulated yield of existing holders.

## Finding Description
`_updateRsETHPrice()` computes:

```solidity
bool isPriceIncreaseOffLimit =
    pricePercentageLimit > 0 &&
    priceDifference > pricePercentageLimit.mulWad(highestRsethPrice);
```

`priceDifference` is the cumulative gap between the current on-chain price and `highestRsethPrice`. There is no `lastUpdateTimestamp` variable, no elapsed-time scaling of the allowed delta, and no early-return when the gap is attributable to normal time passage. The error name `PriceAboveDailyThreshold` and the admin comment ("PricePercentageLimit for 1% is 1e16") confirm the intent is a *per-period* guard, but the implementation is purely cumulative.

When the condition fires, non-manager callers hit `revert PriceAboveDailyThreshold()`. The manager escape hatch `updateRSETHPriceAsManager()` is gated by `onlyLRTManager`, so keeper bots and ordinary EOAs are fully blocked.

During the DoS window, `rsETHPrice` is frozen at its last stored value. `LRTDepositPool.getRsETHAmountToMint()` computes:

```solidity
rsethAmountToMint = (amount * lrtOracle.getAssetPrice(asset)) / lrtOracle.rsETHPrice();
```

A stale (lower) denominator inflates the rsETH minted per unit of deposited ETH. `_beforeDeposit` has no staleness check; deposits proceed normally. Each deposit during the DoS window transfers a fraction of existing holders' accumulated staking yield to the new depositor.

## Impact Explanation
**High — Theft of unclaimed yield.**

Existing rsETH holders have accumulated staking rewards reflected in the true (but unstored) rsETH/ETH rate. New depositors who deposit during the DoS window receive rsETH at the stale lower price, effectively claiming a share of TVL that belongs to existing holders. The excess rsETH minted is a direct, quantifiable transfer of value: at 1% staleness on a 100 ETH deposit, the depositor receives ~1 ETH worth of rsETH that should belong to existing holders. The effect compounds with every deposit made during the window and is irreversible once the rsETH is minted.

## Likelihood Explanation
**Medium.** With `pricePercentageLimit = 1e16` (1%) and ~4% staking APY, the threshold is breached after roughly 90 days of keeper inactivity. Keeper bot failures, infrastructure outages, or misconfiguration are realistic causes. The protocol has no on-chain mechanism to force a price update or to pause deposits automatically when the oracle is stalled; the DoS persists until a manager manually calls `updateRSETHPriceAsManager()`. If the admin sets a tighter limit (e.g., 0.1%), the threshold is breached after ~9 days, significantly increasing likelihood.

## Recommendation
Track the timestamp of the last successful price update and scale the allowed delta by elapsed time:

```solidity
uint256 elapsed = block.timestamp - lastPriceUpdateTimestamp;
uint256 scaledLimit = pricePercentageLimit * elapsed / 1 days;
bool isPriceIncreaseOffLimit =
    pricePercentageLimit > 0 &&
    priceDifference > scaledLimit.mulWad(highestRsethPrice);
```

Additionally, consider pausing deposits automatically (or reverting `getRsETHAmountToMint`) when `rsETHPrice` has not been updated within a configurable staleness window, so that a stalled oracle cannot be exploited by depositors.

## Proof of Concept
1. `pricePercentageLimit` is set to `1e16` (1%). Staking rewards accrue at ~4% APY.
2. The keeper bot stops calling `updateRSETHPrice()` for 91 days. The true rsETH/ETH rate has increased by ~1.0% from `highestRsethPrice`.
3. Any EOA calls `updateRSETHPrice()`. Inside `_updateRsETHPrice()`, `priceDifference > pricePercentageLimit.mulWad(highestRsethPrice)` is `true` and the caller is not a manager → `revert PriceAboveDailyThreshold()`.
4. `rsETHPrice` remains at the 91-day-old stale value.
5. A depositor calls `depositETH(minRSETHAmountExpected, "")`. `getRsETHAmountToMint` computes `(msg.value * assetPrice) / rsETHPrice` using the stale lower `rsETHPrice`, minting ~1% more rsETH than the depositor is entitled to.
6. Existing rsETH holders' proportional claim on protocol TVL is diluted by the excess minted rsETH. Every subsequent deposit during the DoS window compounds the dilution.
7. The DoS continues until a manager calls `updateRSETHPriceAsManager()`.

**Foundry fork test outline:**
- Fork mainnet, deploy/upgrade contracts with `pricePercentageLimit = 1e16`.
- `vm.warp(block.timestamp + 91 days)` to simulate keeper inactivity.
- Call `updateRSETHPrice()` from an EOA; assert it reverts with `PriceAboveDailyThreshold`.
- Record `rsETHPrice` (stale) and compute expected rsETH for a 1 ETH deposit.
- Call `depositETH` and assert minted rsETH exceeds the fair-value amount by ~1%.
- Confirm existing holder's proportional TVL claim has decreased.
