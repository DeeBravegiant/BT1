### Title
Stale `CommitteeMemberResigned` State Persists After CC Re-election via `UpdateCommittee`, Permanently Blocking Hot-Key Registration and Skewing Committee Quorum — (`File: eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/Epoch.hs`)

---

### Summary

When a resigned Constitutional Committee (CC) member is re-added to the committee through an enacted `UpdateCommittee` governance action, the `CommitteeMemberResigned` entry in `CommitteeState` is **not cleared**. This stale flag causes two cascading effects: (1) the re-added member is permanently blocked from registering a hot key (`ConwayCommitteeHasPreviouslyResigned`), and (2) they are silently treated as abstaining in every subsequent ratification calculation, reducing the effective committee denominator and allowing governance actions to reach the threshold with fewer genuine yes-votes than the protocol intends.

---

### Finding Description

**Root cause — `updateCommitteeState` does not clear stale resignation entries for re-added members**

At every epoch boundary the `EPOCH` rule calls `updateCommitteeState` to reconcile `CommitteeState` with the newly enacted committee:

```haskell
-- eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/Epoch.hs
updateCommitteeState :: StrictMaybe (Committee era) -> CommitteeState era -> CommitteeState era
updateCommitteeState committee (CommitteeState creds) =
  CommitteeState $ Map.intersection creds members   -- keeps entries whose cold-key is still in the committee
  where
    members = foldMap' committeeMembers committee
```

`Map.intersection` retains every entry in `creds` whose cold-key appears in the new committee. It **never clears** a `CommitteeMemberResigned` value for a member that was re-added. If a cold credential was previously resigned and is then re-added via `UpdateCommittee` (without first being removed), its `CommitteeMemberResigned` entry survives unchanged.

**Stale flag blocks hot-key registration**

`ConwayAuthCommitteeHotKey` is processed by `checkAndOverwriteCommitteeMemberState` in `GovCert.hs`:

```haskell
-- eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/GovCert.hs
coldCredResigned =
  Map.lookup coldCred csCommitteeCreds >>= \case
    CommitteeMemberResigned {} -> Just coldCred
    CommitteeHotCredential {}  -> Nothing
failOnJust coldCredResigned $ injectFailure . ConwayCommitteeHasPreviouslyResigned
```

This check fires **before** the `isCurrentMember` check. Even though the cold credential is now a legitimate current committee member (re-elected via governance), the stale `CommitteeMemberResigned` entry causes every hot-key registration attempt to fail with `ConwayCommitteeHasPreviouslyResigned`. The member is permanently unable to vote.

**Stale flag silently reduces effective committee size in ratification**

`committeeAcceptedRatio` in `Ratify.hs` treats resigned members as abstaining:

```haskell
-- eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/Ratify.hs
Just (CommitteeMemberResigned _) -> (yes, tot)  -- member has resigned, vote "abstain"
```

A re-added member who is still marked resigned is excluded from both the numerator and denominator of the acceptance ratio. With a committee of N members where K are stale-resigned, the effective denominator shrinks to N−K, making the threshold easier to reach.

**The test suite documents this as a known behavioral quirk**

```haskell
-- eras/conway/impl/testlib/Test/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Imp/EnactSpec.hs
-- A CC that has resigned will need to be first voted out and then voted in to be considered active
it "CC re-election" $ do
  ...
  passNEpochs 2
  -- Confirm that they are still resigned
  ccShouldBeResigned cc
```

The workaround (remove then re-add in two separate governance proposals) is non-obvious and not enforced by the protocol.

---

### Impact Explanation

A governance actor who controls or can influence a CC member can:

1. Have the CC member resign.
2. Propose an `UpdateCommittee` action that re-adds the same cold credential (without removing it first).
3. After enactment, the re-added member is permanently unable to vote and is silently discounted from quorum.
4. Subsequent governance proposals (parameter changes, treasury withdrawals, hard-fork initiations, constitution updates) can now be ratified with fewer genuine CC yes-votes than the protocol threshold requires.

This constitutes an **unauthorized governance action being enacted**: the enacted committee composition appears correct on-chain, but the effective voting power is lower than the threshold mandates, allowing proposals to pass that should have failed.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- Any CC member can unilaterally resign (a single transaction).
- Re-adding a resigned member via `UpdateCommittee` without first removing them is a natural governance operation that the community may attempt in good faith.
- The `UpdateCommittee` proposal requires DRep + SPO ratification, but an attacker who controls a CC seat and has moderate DRep/SPO support can execute this path.
- The behavior is not surfaced as an error — the re-added member simply appears in the committee with no hot key, indistinguishable from a member who has not yet registered.

Likelihood: **Medium**.

---

### Recommendation

In `updateCommitteeState`, after computing `Map.intersection`, additionally clear `CommitteeMemberResigned` entries for members whose cold credential appears in the `membersToAdd` set of the most recently enacted `UpdateCommittee` action. Concretely, the epoch transition should pass the enacted `membersToAdd` set to `updateCommitteeState` and delete those keys from the resigned-member map before the intersection:

```haskell
updateCommitteeState committee newlyAddedMembers (CommitteeState creds) =
  CommitteeState $
    Map.intersection
      (creds `Map.withoutKeys` newlyAddedMembers)  -- clear stale resignation for re-added members
      members
  where
    members = foldMap' committeeMembers committee
```

Alternatively, the `ConwayCommitteeHasPreviouslyResigned` guard in `checkAndOverwriteCommitteeMemberState` should be conditioned on the member **not** being a current committee member, so that a legitimately re-elected member can register a new hot key.

### Proof of Concept

```
Epoch 0:
  CC member `cc` is elected (cold key in committee, hot key registered).

Epoch 1:
  `cc` submits ResignCommitteeColdKey → CommitteeState[cc] = CommitteeMemberResigned.

Epoch 2:
  Governance proposal: UpdateCommittee {} {} {cc → epoch+20} threshold
  (re-adds cc without removing it first)
  DRep + SPO votes pass. Proposal enacted at epoch boundary.

Epoch 3:
  updateCommitteeState: cc is in new committee → CommitteeMemberResigned entry preserved.
  cc attempts AuthCommitteeHotKey → FAILS: ConwayCommitteeHasPreviouslyResigned.
  cc is counted as abstaining in committeeAcceptedRatio.
  Effective committee denominator = N−1.
  Any proposal requiring CC threshold T now needs ⌈T·(N−1)⌉ yes-votes instead of ⌈T·N⌉.
```
