### Title
Deposit Refund in Meta Transactions Sent to Delegate Sender (Alice) Instead of Relayer Who Paid — (`runtime/runtime/src/actions.rs`)

### Summary

In NEAR meta transactions, when an inner receipt fails, the deposit refund is sent to the delegate action sender (`predecessor_id` = Alice) rather than the relayer (`signer_id`) who actually funded the deposit. This is the direct NEAR analog of the Wormhole M-19 bug: using the immediate caller (`msg.sender` / `predecessor_id`) as the default refund destination instead of the original payer (`tx.origin` / `signer_id`).

### Finding Description

In `apply_delegate_action` (`runtime/runtime/src/actions.rs`, lines 456–469), the inner receipt spawned from a `DelegateAction` is constructed as a `ReceiptEnum::Action` (V0) with:

- `predecessor_id: sender_id.clone()` — Alice, the delegate action sender
- `signer_id: action_receipt.signer_id().clone()` — the relayer, who purchased all gas and funded all attached deposits [1](#0-0) 

Because the receipt is `ReceiptEnum::Action` (V0), `refund_to()` unconditionally returns `&None`: [2](#0-1) 

So `balance_refund_receiver()` falls back to `predecessor_id` (Alice): [3](#0-2) 

When the inner receipt fails, `refund_unspent_gas_and_deposits` sends the deposit refund to `balance_refund_receiver()` — Alice — not the relayer: [4](#0-3) 

The code comment at lines 471–475 explicitly acknowledges this:

> "Note, Relayer prepaid all fees and all things required by actions: attached deposits and attached gas. If something goes wrong, deposit is refunded to the predecessor, this is sender_id/Sender in DelegateAction. … Therefore Relayer should verify DelegateAction before submitting it because it spends the attached deposit." [5](#0-4) 

The architecture documentation also confirms the financial risk:

> "there is a financial incentive for Alice to submit meta transactions that have high balances attached but will fail on Bob's shard." [6](#0-5) 

### Impact Explanation

The relayer pays for all attached deposits in a meta transaction. When the inner receipt fails, the deposit is refunded to Alice (the delegate sender / `predecessor_id`) rather than the relayer (`signer_id`) who funded it. This directly corrupts the balance state: Alice's account balance increases by the refunded deposit amount, while the relayer's account never recovers the funds it spent. The corrupted value is the on-chain `amount` field of Alice's account after the refund receipt executes.

### Likelihood Explanation

Any unprivileged user (Alice) who can submit a `SignedDelegateAction` to a relayer can trigger this. Alice crafts a meta transaction with a large `deposit` attached to a function call that is guaranteed to fail on the receiver's shard (e.g., calling a non-existent method, or a method that panics). The relayer, acting as a permissionless service, submits the transaction. The inner receipt fails, and the deposit is refunded to Alice. No validator, node admin, or special privilege is required on Alice's part.

### Recommendation

In `apply_delegate_action`, construct the inner receipt as `ReceiptEnum::ActionV2` (which carries a `refund_to` field) and set `refund_to: Some(action_receipt.signer_id().clone())`. This routes deposit refunds to the relayer (the actual payer) rather than Alice, mirroring the fix in the Wormhole report of using `tx.origin` instead of `msg.sender`.

### Proof of Concept

1. Alice crafts a `DelegateAction` targeting `receiver_id = bob.near`, with a `FunctionCall` action attaching a large deposit (e.g., 100 NEAR) to a method that does not exist on `bob.near`.
2. Alice signs the `DelegateAction` and sends it off-chain to a relayer.
3. The relayer wraps it in a transaction (relayer is `signer_id`), paying for all gas and the 100 NEAR deposit.
4. On Alice's shard, `apply_delegate_action` creates the inner receipt with `predecessor_id = alice.near`, `signer_id = relayer.near`, and no `refund_to`.
5. On Bob's shard, the function call fails (method not found). `refund_unspent_gas_and_deposits` calls `receipt.balance_refund_receiver()` → returns `alice.near` (predecessor).
6. A `new_balance_refund` receipt is generated sending 100 NEAR to `alice.near`.
7. Alice's balance increases by 100 NEAR; the relayer never recovers the deposit it paid.
