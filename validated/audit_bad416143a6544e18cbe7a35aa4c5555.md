Audit Report

## Title
Missing `isEthDepositEnabled` Guard in `RSETHPoolV3ExternalBridge.deposit()` Bypasses Protocol-Wide ETH Deposit Disable — (File: contracts/pools/RSETHPoolV3ExternalBridge.sol)

## Summary
`RSETHPoolV3ExternalBridge` omits the `isEthDepositEnabled` state variable and its corresponding guard that both sibling contracts `RSETHPoolV3` and `RSETHPoolV3WithNativeChainBridge` enforce at the top of their ETH `deposit` paths. When the protocol operator disables ETH deposits across the other two pools, any unprivileged depositor can still call `RSETHPoolV3ExternalBridge.deposit()` and receive freshly minted wrsETH, defeating the protocol's administrative control surface.

## Finding Description
`RSETHPoolV3` declares `bool public isEthDepositEnabled` and enforces it in `deposit(string)`: [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) 

`RSETHPoolV3WithNativeChainBridge` mirrors this exactly: [3](#0-2) [4](#0-3) 

`RSETHPoolV3ExternalBridge` has no such variable anywhere in its storage layout (lines 42–103) and its `deposit(string)` function proceeds directly to minting after only checking `whenNotPaused` and `limitDailyMint`: [5](#0-4) 

The `whenNotPaused` and `limitDailyMint` guards are insufficient substitutes: `whenNotPaused` is a coarser control that also blocks token deposits and bridging operations, and `limitDailyMint` only caps the daily volume — neither replicates the targeted ETH-deposit-only disable that `isEthDepositEnabled` provides.

## Impact Explanation
**Low — Contract fails to deliver promised returns.** The protocol exposes `setIsEthDepositEnabled` on both sibling pools precisely to allow targeted, independent disabling of ETH deposits (e.g., during an oracle rate anomaly or a planned upgrade). `RSETHPoolV3ExternalBridge` does not honor this control: when the flag is set to `false` on the other pools, ETH deposits through this contract continue unimpeded, minting wrsETH at whatever rate the oracle currently reports. Existing wrsETH holders may be diluted if the disable was triggered by an unfavorable oracle rate. The protocol cannot stop this without invoking the coarser `pause()`, which also halts token deposits and bridging.

## Likelihood Explanation
No special privilege is required. Any depositor who is aware of the discrepancy — or who simply tries the pool while the others are disabled — can call `RSETHPoolV3ExternalBridge.deposit{value: X}("")` and succeed. The `isEthDepositEnabled` flag is already wired into two of the three V3-family pools, making the omission in the third a straightforward copy-paste divergence that is easy to discover by comparing the contracts.

## Recommendation
Add `bool public isEthDepositEnabled` to the storage layout of `RSETHPoolV3ExternalBridge` and insert the guard at the top of `deposit(string memory referralId)`, mirroring the pattern in `RSETHPoolV3`:

```solidity
if (!isEthDepositEnabled) revert EthDepositDisabled();
```

Add the `EthDepositDisabled` custom error and a `setIsEthDepositEnabled(bool)` setter gated by `TIMELOCK_ROLE`, consistent with `RSETHPoolV3.setIsEthDepositEnabled` and `RSETHPoolV3WithNativeChainBridge.setIsEthDepositEnabled`. [6](#0-5) 

## Proof of Concept
1. Deploy or fork with `RSETHPoolV3`, `RSETHPoolV3WithNativeChainBridge`, and `RSETHPoolV3ExternalBridge` all pointing to the same wrsETH token.
2. Call `RSETHPoolV3.setIsEthDepositEnabled(false)` and `RSETHPoolV3WithNativeChainBridge.setIsEthDepositEnabled(false)` from the `TIMELOCK_ROLE` address.
3. Confirm both pools revert with `EthDepositDisabled` on `deposit{value: 1 ether}("")`.
4. Call `RSETHPoolV3ExternalBridge.deposit{value: 1 ether}("")` from any EOA.
5. Observe the call succeeds and `wrsETH.mint` executes, issuing wrsETH at the current oracle rate while the protocol intended ETH deposits to be disabled.

Foundry fork test: assert that after step 2, `RSETHPoolV3ExternalBridge.deposit{value: 1 ether}("")` does **not** revert, and that the caller's wrsETH balance increases — confirming the bypass. [5](#0-4)
