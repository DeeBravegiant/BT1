Audit Report

## Title
Nested Unbounded Loops in `updateRSETHPrice()` Cause Excessive Gas Consumption, Risking Price Oracle Freeze — (File: `contracts/LRTOracle.sol`, `contracts/LRTDepositPool.sol`, `contracts/NodeDelegator.sol`)

## Summary
The public `updateRSETHPrice()` function in `LRTOracle` triggers a deeply nested multi-contract call chain whose gas cost scales as N assets × M NDCs × Q queued withdrawals × S strategies per withdrawal. The protocol's internal cap of 80 total queued withdrawals is a global counter that can be bypassed by EigenLayer-side forced undelegations, and even within the cap the loop structure makes `getAssetUnstaking` call `getQueuedWithdrawals` N×M times (up to 100 external fetches of the full withdrawal list). If the function runs out of gas, `rsETHPrice` becomes permanently stale, disrupting all deposit and withdrawal pricing.

## Finding Description

**Call chain (all confirmed in code):**

`LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()` (public, `LRTOracle.sol:87`) → `_updateRsETHPrice()` → `_getTotalEthInProtocol()` (`LRTOracle.sol:331–349`) loops over every supported asset and calls `ILRTDepositPool.getTotalAssetDeposits(asset)` → `getAssetDistributionData(asset)` (`LRTDepositPool.sol:446–456`) loops over every NDC and calls `INodeDelegator.getAssetUnstaking(asset)` → `NodeDelegator.getAssetUnstaking()` (`NodeDelegator.sol:405–427`) calls `_getDelegationManager().getQueuedWithdrawals(address(this))` and iterates over all returned withdrawals and their strategies.

**Root cause — redundant full-list fetches:**
`getAssetUnstaking` is invoked once per (asset, NDC) pair. With N=10 assets and M=10 NDCs, `getQueuedWithdrawals` is called 100 times. Each call returns the full withdrawal list for that NDC. The inner loops then iterate over all Q withdrawals × S strategies to filter by asset. The total strategy-level iterations are N × Σ_j(Q_j × S_j). With the global cap of 80 total withdrawals spread across 10 NDCs and S=2 strategies, the worst case under normal operation is 10 × 80 × 2 = 1,600 strategy iterations plus 100 cold external calls to `getQueuedWithdrawals`.

**Why the existing cap is insufficient:**
`LRTUnstakingVault.maxUncompletedWithdrawalCount` is capped at 80 (`LRTUnstakingVault.sol:153`). However, `getAssetUnstaking` queries EigenLayer's `getQueuedWithdrawals` directly, which reflects EigenLayer's on-chain state, not the protocol's internal counter. EigenLayer operators can force undelegation of an NDC at any time, creating new queued withdrawals on EigenLayer's side that are not tracked by `uncompletedWithdrawalCount` until a privileged manager calls `setUncompletedWithdrawalCount()`. During this window, the actual withdrawal count returned by `getQueuedWithdrawals` can exceed 80, and the protocol's guard in `initiateUnstaking` / `undelegate` does not prevent this external-origin spike. The protocol's own comment acknowledges this: *"Need buffer for theoretical operator forced undelegations (ndc count * asset count = 15)"* (`LRTUnstakingVault.sol:152`), yet the cap is set at 80 with no on-chain enforcement against EigenLayer-side additions.

**Same loop triggered on every deposit:**
`_checkIfDepositAmountExceedesCurrentLimit` → `getTotalAssetDeposits` (`LRTDepositPool.sol:676–682`) executes the same nested loop on every user deposit, meaning gas exhaustion also blocks deposits.

## Impact Explanation

If `updateRSETHPrice()` reverts out-of-gas, `rsETHPrice` in `LRTOracle` becomes permanently stale. All downstream consumers — `getRsETHAmountToMint` (deposit minting), `getExpectedAssetAmount` (withdrawal sizing), and withdrawal queue unlocking — operate on an incorrect price. This constitutes **Medium — Unbounded gas consumption**, with a secondary **Medium — Temporary freezing of funds** if the stale price causes deposit or withdrawal operations to revert on limit or slippage checks.

## Likelihood Explanation

No attacker action is required. The gas exhaustion emerges from the protocol's own scaling: 10 NDCs, 10 supported assets, and up to 80 queued withdrawals are all within normal operational parameters. EigenLayer forced undelegations — a standard EigenLayer mechanism, not an attack — can push the actual queued withdrawal count above the protocol's tracked value without any privileged action by the protocol team. The public `updateRSETHPrice()` function can be called by anyone, and the OOG condition is repeatable until the withdrawal queue drains or a manager intervenes.

## Recommendation

1. **Eliminate redundant `getQueuedWithdrawals` calls**: Cache the result of `getQueuedWithdrawals` per NDC once and reuse it across all asset iterations, reducing the 100 external fetches to 10.
2. **Maintain an incremental accounting variable**: Replace live EigenLayer enumeration in `getAssetUnstaking` with a stored `assetUnstakingAmount[asset]` mapping updated on `initiateUnstaking` / `completeUnstaking` events, removing the dependency on `getQueuedWithdrawals` entirely during price updates.
3. **Bound `getQueuedWithdrawals` iteration**: Add a hard iteration limit in `getAssetUnstaking` equal to `maxUncompletedWithdrawalCount` and revert or return a sentinel value if exceeded, preventing silent OOG.
4. **Resync counter on forced undelegation**: Emit an event or auto-call `setUncompletedWithdrawalCount` when a forced undelegation is detected, so the protocol's counter stays accurate without requiring manual manager intervention.
