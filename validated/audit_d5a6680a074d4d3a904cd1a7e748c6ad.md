### Title
Bootstrap-Phase DRep Re-delegation Omits Reverse-Delegation Cleanup, Enabling Governance Vote Suppression via DRep Unregistration — (`eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/Deleg.hs`)

---

### Summary

In the Conway bootstrap phase (protocol version 9, `pvMajor pv < natVersion @10`), `processDelegationInternal` intentionally skips removing the old reverse-delegation entry from a DRep's `drepDelegs` set when a stake credential re-delegates to a new credential DRep. This is the direct analog of the Velodrome `_burn()` omission: delegation is updated on registration and on transfer, but the reverse-delegation index is not cleaned up on the equivalent of "burn" (re-delegation away). The stale `drepDelegs` entries are then consumed by `ConwayUnRegDRep` in `GovCert.hs`, which unconditionally clears `dRepDelegationAccountStateL` for every credential listed in the departing DRep's `drepDelegs` — including credentials that have already moved to a different DRep. This silently strips those credentials of their active DRep delegation, removing their stake from the ratification distribution and suppressing the voting power of the DRep they legitimately moved to.

---

### Finding Description

**Root cause — `processDelegationInternal` (Deleg.hs lines 332–377)**

When `preserveIncorrectDelegation` is `True` (bootstrap phase), the `delegVote` branch only inserts the stake credential into the new DRep's `drepDelegs` set; it never calls `unDelegReDelegDRep` to remove it from the old DRep's set:

```haskell
| isNothing mAccountState || preserveIncorrectDelegation ->
    certVStateL . vsDRepsL
      %~ Map.adjust (drepDelegsL %~ Set.insert stakeCred) dRepCred
```

The account-level forward pointer (`dRepDelegationAccountStateL`) is correctly updated to the new DRep, but the reverse index (`drepDelegs` inside `DRepState`) for the old DRep is left stale.

**Propagation — `ConwayUnRegDRep` (GovCert.hs lines 234–254)**

When the old DRep later unregisters, the handler reads its `drepDelegs` set and blindly clears the delegation field for every listed credential:

```haskell
clearDRepDelegations delegs accountsMap =
  foldr (Map.adjust (dRepDelegationAccountStateL .~ Nothing)) accountsMap delegs
```

Because `drepDelegs` still contains credentials that have already re-delegated to a second DRep, those credentials have their `dRepDelegationAccountStateL` set to `Nothing`, even though the account state correctly pointed to the new DRep.

**Ratification impact — `computeDRepDistr` (DRepPulser.hs lines 228–241)**

The DRep stake distribution used for ratification is built from `accountState ^. dRepDelegationAccountStateL`. After the spurious clear, that field is `Nothing`, so `addToDRepDistr` returns the accumulator unchanged — the credential's stake is not added to any DRep's distribution. The second DRep loses that voting weight silently.

**Test confirmation — DelegSpec.hs lines 307–323**

The test suite explicitly documents and asserts this behavior in bootstrap mode:

```haskell
ifBootstrap
  ( do
      accounts <- getsNES $ nesEsL . esLStateL . lsCertStateL . certDStateL . accountsL
      expectNothingExpr (lookupDRepDelegation cred accounts)  -- delegation wiped
      expecteReverseDRepDelegation cred drepCred2 True        -- stale entry still present
  )
  (expectDelegatedVote cred (DRepCredential drepCred2))
```

**Secondary impact — `queryRegisteredDRepStakeDistr` (Query.hs lines 259–274)**

This query computes DRep stake by iterating `drepDelegs` directly. In bootstrap phase, the stale entries cause the old DRep's reported stake to be inflated (double-counting credentials that have moved), misleading wallets and governance tooling about actual voting power.

---

### Impact Explanation

An attacker who operates DRep A can suppress the governance voting power of any DRep B that organic delegators migrate to:

1. Attacker registers DRep A and attracts delegations (or uses their own stake credentials).
2. Delegators re-delegate to DRep B. In bootstrap phase, DRep A's `drepDelegs` retains all those credentials.
3. Attacker unregisters DRep A (recovering the deposit). `clearDRepDelegations` wipes `dRepDelegationAccountStateL` for every credential in the stale set.
4. DRep B's effective stake in `reDRepDistr` drops by the sum of all affected credentials' stake.
5. Governance proposals that DRep B would have ratified may now fail; proposals that DRep B would have blocked may now pass.

This matches **Critical — Unauthorized governance, treasury, protocol-parameter, committee, constitution, or hard-fork action is enacted**, because suppressing a legitimate DRep's voting weight can tip ratification thresholds for `ParameterChange`, `TreasuryWithdrawals`, `HardForkInitiation`, `UpdateCommittee`, and `NewConstitution` proposals.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- The bootstrap phase is active on any node running Conway at protocol version 9 (major version < 10). The hard-fork to PV10 has not yet occurred on mainnet at the time of this analysis.
- DRep registration, delegation, re-delegation, and unregistration are all standard, permissionless, unprivileged certificate operations available to any transaction sender.
- The attacker needs only to register a DRep (one deposit), attract or self-generate delegations, wait for re-delegations to a target DRep, and then unregister. No privileged access, key compromise, or supermajority is required.
- The `updateDRepDelegations` cleanup in `HardFork.hs` (lines 82–105) only runs at the PV9→PV10 transition; until that transition occurs, the stale entries persist and the attack window is open.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Immediate**: In `processDelegationInternal`, remove the `preserveIncorrectDelegation` guard for the reverse-delegation removal path, or — if backward compatibility must be preserved — add a compensating check in `ConwayUnRegDRep` that skips clearing `dRepDelegationAccountStateL` for any credential whose current `dRepDelegationAccountStateL` no longer points to the departing DRep.
2. **Structural**: The `clearDRepDelegations` helper in `GovCert.hs` should verify that each credential in `drepDelegs` still actually delegates to the departing DRep before clearing it:

```haskell
clearDRepDelegations cred delegs accountsMap =
  foldr
    (\stakeCred m ->
       Map.adjust
         (\as -> if as ^. dRepDelegationAccountStateL == Just (DRepCredential cred)
                 then as & dRepDelegationAccountStateL .~ Nothing
                 else as)
         stakeCred m)
    accountsMap
    delegs
```

3. **Long-term**: Accelerate the PV9→PV10 hard fork so that `updateDRepDelegations` can reconcile all stale `drepDelegs` entries.

### Proof of Concept

**Step 1 — Bootstrap phase (PV9). Register DRep A and stake credential S, delegate S to DRep A.**

```
Tx1: [RegDRepTxCert drepA deposit, RegDepositDelegTxCert credS (DelegVote (DRepCredential drepA)) keyDeposit]
```

State: `drepA.drepDelegs = {credS}`, `credS.dRepDelegationAccountStateL = Just drepA`

**Step 2 — Re-delegate S to DRep B.**

```
Tx2: [DelegTxCert credS (DelegVote (DRepCredential drepB))]
```

Post-bootstrap state (correct): `drepA.drepDelegs = {}`, `drepB.drepDelegs = {credS}`, `credS.dRepDelegationAccountStateL = Just drepB`

Bootstrap state (buggy, `preserveIncorrectDelegation = True`): `drepA.drepDelegs = {credS}` ← stale, `drepB.drepDelegs = {credS}`, `credS.dRepDelegationAccountStateL = Just drepB`

**Step 3 — Unregister DRep A.**

```
Tx3: [UnRegDRepTxCert drepA deposit]
```

`clearDRepDelegations {credS} accountsMap` sets `credS.dRepDelegationAccountStateL = Nothing`.

**Step 4 — Observe governance impact.**

`computeDRepDistr` now finds `credS.dRepDelegationAccountStateL = Nothing` → credS's stake is not added to drepB's distribution. DRep B's ratification weight is reduced by credS's full stake + rewards + proposal deposits.

This is confirmed by the existing test assertion at `DelegSpec.hs:320`:

processDelegationInternal ::
  ConwayEraCertState era =>
  -- | Preserve the buggy behavior where DRep delegations are not updated correctly (See #4772)
  Bool ->
  -- | Delegator
  Credential Staking ->
  -- | Account state for the above stake credential
  Maybe (AccountState era) ->
  -- | New delegatee
  Delegatee ->
  CertState era ->
  CertState era
processDelegationInternal preserveIncorrectDelegation stakeCred mAccountState newDelegatee =
  case newDelegatee of
    DelegStake sPool -> delegStake sPool
    DelegVote dRep -> delegVote dRep
    DelegStakeVote sPool dRep -> delegVote dRep . delegStake sPool
  where
    delegStake stakePool cState =
      cState
        & certDStateL . accountsL
          %~ adjustAccountState (stakePoolDelegationAccountStateL ?~ stakePool) stakeCred
        & maybe
          (certPStateL . psStakePoolsL %~ Map.adjust (spsDelegatorsL %~ Set.insert stakeCred) stakePool)
          (\accountState -> certPStateL %~ unDelegReDelegStakePool stakeCred accountState (Just stakePool))
          mAccountState

    delegVote dRep cState =
      let handleReverseDelegation =
            case dRepToCred dRep of
              Just dRepCred
                -- This is the case where we only add the new reverse delegation and do not remove
                -- the old one, which is the behavior that we want:
                --
                -- 1) for new accounts, since there is no old reverse delegation to remove
                --
                -- 2) in the bootstrap phase, in order to preserve the incorrect behavior, where old reverse
                --   delegation for the prior DRep was wrongfully retained. It is important to note
                --   that in case when the new delegation was to a predefined DRep, the reverse
                --   delegations where handled correctly even in the boostrap phase
                --
                -- For reference here is the original bug report:
                --   https://github.com/IntersectMBO/cardano-ledger/issues/4772
                | isNothing mAccountState || preserveIncorrectDelegation ->
                    certVStateL . vsDRepsL
                      %~ Map.adjust (drepDelegsL %~ Set.insert stakeCred) dRepCred

-- | Reverses DRep delegation.
-- To be called when a stake credential is unregistered or its delegation target changes.
-- If the new delegation matches the previous one, this is a noop.
unDelegReDelegDRep ::
  ConwayEraAccounts era =>
  Credential Staking ->
  -- | Account that is losing its current delegation and/or acquiring a new one
  AccountState era ->
  -- | Potential new delegation. In case when stake credential unregisters this must be `Nothing`.
  Maybe DRep ->
  VState era ->
  VState era
unDelegReDelegDRep stakeCred accountState mNewDRep =
  fromMaybe (vsDRepsL %~ addNewDelegation) $ do
    dRep@(DRepCredential dRepCred) <- accountState ^. dRepDelegationAccountStateL
    pure $
      -- There is no need to update set of delegations if delegation is unchanged
      if Just dRep == mNewDRep
        then id
        else
          vsDRepsL %~ addNewDelegation . Map.adjust (drepDelegsL %~ Set.delete stakeCred) dRepCred
  where
    addNewDelegation =
      case mNewDRep of
        Just (DRepCredential dRepCred) ->
          Map.adjust (drepDelegsL %~ Set.insert stakeCred) dRepCred
        _ -> id
```

updateDRepDelegations :: ConwayEraCertState era => CertState era -> CertState era
updateDRepDelegations certState =
  let accountsMap = certState ^. certDStateL . accountsL . accountsMapL
      dReps =
        -- Reset all delegations in order to remove any inconsistencies
        -- Delegations will be reset accordingly below.
        Map.map (\dRepState -> dRepState {drepDelegs = Set.empty}) $
          certState ^. certVStateL . vsDRepsL
      (dRepsWithDelegations, accountsWithoutUnknownDRepDelegations) =
        Map.mapAccumWithKey adjustDelegations dReps accountsMap
      adjustDelegations ds stakeCred accountState =
        case accountState ^. dRepDelegationAccountStateL of
          Just (DRepCredential dRep) ->
            let addDelegation _ dRepState =
                  Just $ dRepState {drepDelegs = Set.insert stakeCred (drepDelegs dRepState)}
             in case Map.updateLookupWithKey addDelegation dRep ds of
                  (Nothing, _) -> (ds, accountState & dRepDelegationAccountStateL .~ Nothing)
                  (Just _, ds') -> (ds', accountState)
          _ -> (ds, accountState)
   in certState
        -- Remove dangling delegations to non-existent DReps:
        & certDStateL . accountsL . accountsMapL .~ accountsWithoutUnknownDRepDelegations
        -- Populate DRep delegations with delegatees
        & certVStateL . vsDRepsL .~ dRepsWithDelegations
```

queryRegisteredDRepStakeDistr nes creds =
  Map.foldlWithKey' computeDistr mempty selectedDReps
  where
    selectedDReps
      | null creds = registeredDReps
      | otherwise = registeredDReps `Map.restrictKeys` creds
    registeredDReps = nes ^. nesEsL . esLStateL . lsCertStateL . certVStateL . vsDRepsL
    computeDistr distrAcc dRepCred (DRepState {..}) =
      Map.insert dRepCred (totalDelegations drepDelegs) distrAcc
    totalDelegations =
      fromCompact . foldMap stakeAndDeposits
    instantStake = nes ^. instantStakeL . instantStakeCredentialsL
    proposalDeposits = proposalsDeposits $ nes ^. newEpochStateGovStateL . proposalsGovStateL
    stakeAndDeposits stakeCred =
      fromMaybe (CompactCoin 0) $
        Map.lookup stakeCred instantStake <> Map.lookup stakeCred proposalDeposits
