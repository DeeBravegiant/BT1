### Title
Native ETH Push in `finTransfer` Permanently Locks Sender Funds When Recipient Contract Reverts - (File: evm/src/omni-bridge/contracts/OmniBridge.sol)

### Summary
`OmniBridge.finTransfer()` delivers native ETH to the recipient via a direct `.call{value:}`. If the recipient is a contract that reverts on ETH receipt, the delivery permanently fails. Because the NEAR-side transfer is already committed (tokens locked/burned), and no cancel mechanism exists to recover them, the sender's funds are irreversibly locked.

### Finding Description
In `OmniBridge.finTransfer()`, when `payload.tokenAddress == address(0)` (native ETH transfer), the contract pushes ETH directly to `payload.recipient`:

```solidity
(bool success, ) = payload.recipient.call{value: payload.amount}("");
if (!success) revert FailedToSendEther();
``` [1](#0-0) 

There is no pull/claim pattern. If `payload.recipient` is a contract whose `receive()` or `fallback()` reverts, every call to `finTransfer` for that transfer will revert with `FailedToSendEther`, making the transfer permanently undeliverable on the EVM side.

The nonce is marked used before the push:

```solidity
completedTransfers[payload.destinationNonce] = true;
``` [2](#0-1) 

Because the entire transaction reverts when the ETH push fails, the nonce is not permanently consumed — the relayer can retry. However, retrying is futile if the recipient contract always reverts. Meanwhile, the NEAR-side transfer is already committed: tokens are burned or locked in `init_transfer_internal` before the cross-chain message is emitted. [3](#0-2) 

No public cancel function was found in the NEAR bridge contract that would allow the sender to recover locked/burned tokens after a transfer has been initiated. The `remove_transfer_message` helper is internal and not exposed as a user-callable cancel. [4](#0-3) 

### Impact Explanation
A sender (Alice) initiates a NEAR→EVM ETH transfer specifying Bob's upgradeable contract as the EVM recipient. Bob upgrades his contract to reject ETH. Every subsequent `finTransfer` call reverts. Alice's ETH is burned/locked on NEAR with no recovery path. This is an irreversible fund lock matching the Critical impact category: "Irreversible fund lock, frozen redemption path, or permanently unclaimable user or protocol value in bridge, token, fee, vault, fast-transfer, or UTXO flows."

### Likelihood Explanation
Low. The attack requires the recipient to be an upgradeable contract that is later modified to reject ETH. Accidental triggering (e.g., a contract without a `receive` function) is also possible and constitutes a non-intentional permanent fund lock. The path is reachable through the public `finTransfer` entrypoint with no privileged access required.

### Recommendation
Replace the direct ETH push with a pull/claim pattern:

1. When the ETH push fails, store the pending amount in a `mapping(address => uint256) public pendingWithdrawals` instead of reverting.
2. Mark the nonce as completed regardless.
3. Expose a `claimETH()` function that lets the recipient withdraw their pending balance.

This mirrors the recommendation in the external report and eliminates the griefing vector entirely.

### Proof of Concept
1. Alice calls NEAR bridge `init_transfer` with `recipient = Bob's EVM contract address`, sending 1 ETH. Tokens are locked on NEAR.
2. Bob upgrades his EVM contract so its `receive()` function always reverts.
3. Relayer calls `OmniBridge.finTransfer(sig, payload)` on EVM.
4. Execution reaches line 319: `payload.recipient.call{value: 1 ether}("")` — Bob's contract reverts.
5. `FailedToSendEther` is thrown; the entire EVM transaction reverts.
6. Relayer retries indefinitely — all attempts revert.
7. Alice's 1 ETH remains locked on NEAR with no cancel path. Funds are permanently unclaimable. [5](#0-4)
