Audit Report

## Title
Unbounded Gas Growth in Deposit Path via Nested NDC and EigenLayer Withdrawal Iteration - (File: `contracts/LRTDepositPool.sol`)

## Summary
Every call to `depositETH()` or `depositAsset()` triggers a full traversal of all NodeDelegators and their EigenLayer queued withdrawals through the `_beforeDeposit` → `getTotalAssetDeposits` → `getAssetDistributionData`/`getETHDistributionData` → `getAssetUnstaking` call chain. Gas cost scales as O(NDC_count × queued_withdrawals_per_NDC × strategies_per_withdrawal), and since `maxNodeDelegatorLimit` has no hard upper bound, this can grow to the point of reverting all deposits.

## Finding Description
The confirmed execution path on every deposit:

1. `depositETH()` / `depositAsset()` call `_beforeDeposit()` which checks deposit limits via `getTotalAssetDeposits()`.
2. `getTotalAssetDeposits()` calls `getAssetDistributionData()`, which for ETH delegates to `getETHDistributionData()`.
3. Both distribution functions loop over the entire `nodeDelegatorQueue` array and call `getAssetUnstaking()` on each NDC.
4. `getAssetUnstaking()` in `NodeDelegator.sol` calls `_getDelegationManager().getQueuedWithdrawals(address(this))` — a full external call to EigenLayer — and then performs a **nested loop** over all returned withdrawal structs and their strategies.

The total gas per deposit scales as:
```
nodeDelegatorQueue.length × (3 external calls + queuedWithdrawals_per_NDC × strategies_per_withdrawal)
```

`updateMaxNodeDelegatorLimit()` only enforces `maxNodeDelegatorLimit_ >= nodeDelegatorQueue.length` with no hard ceiling, so an admin can raise it to any value. `maxUncompletedWithdrawalCount` is capped at 80 total across all NDCs, but this cap is per-protocol, not per-NDC, and the iteration still touches all of them on every deposit. The protocol's own comment in `LRTUnstakingVault.sol` acknowledges that 120 uncompleted withdrawals is the ceiling for price updates to still function — confirming the team is aware of gas sensitivity in this pattern — yet no analogous guard exists on the deposit path.

## Impact Explanation
**Medium — Unbounded gas consumption / Temporary freezing of funds.** At 10 NDCs with 80 total queued withdrawals (each with multiple strategies), every deposit already incurs 10 external `getQueuedWithdrawals()` calls plus nested iteration. If `maxNodeDelegatorLimit` is raised to 20–30 as the protocol expands (an expected operational activity), gas cost scales linearly. At sufficient scale, `depositETH()` and `depositAsset()` revert with out-of-gas, temporarily freezing all new user deposits. This matches the allowed impact "Medium. Temporary freezing of funds" and "Medium. Unbounded gas consumption."

## Likelihood Explanation
Triggered on **every** user deposit — no attacker action required. The gas cost grows monotonically as the protocol adds NDCs and queues more EigenLayer withdrawals, both of which are normal operational activities. Any unprivileged depositor is affected. The likelihood increases over time as the protocol scales.

## Recommendation
Decouple the EigenLayer queued withdrawal accounting from the hot deposit path:
- Maintain a cached `assetUnstaking` value per NDC updated lazily only when `initiateUnstaking` or `completeUnstaking` is called, rather than recomputing via live EigenLayer queries on every deposit.
- Alternatively, use a stored TVL snapshot for deposit limit checks and restrict the live `getTotalAssetDeposits()` computation to view-only off-chain calls.

## Proof of Concept
1. Admin adds 10 NDCs to `nodeDelegatorQueue` (`maxNodeDelegatorLimit = 10`).
2. Protocol queues 80 EigenLayer withdrawals across all NDCs (`maxUncompletedWithdrawalCount = 80`), each with 2 strategies.
3. Any user calls `depositETH(1 ether, 0)`.
4. Execution: `depositETH` → `_beforeDeposit` → `getTotalAssetDeposits` → `getETHDistributionData` → 10 iterations, each invoking `getAssetUnstaking(ETH_TOKEN)` → 10 external `getQueuedWithdrawals()` calls to EigenLayer + nested iteration over ~8 withdrawal structs × 2 strategies per NDC.
5. Admin raises `maxNodeDelegatorLimit` to 20 and adds 10 more NDCs; gas cost doubles.
6. At sufficient NDC count and queued withdrawal accumulation, the transaction reverts with out-of-gas, blocking all new deposits.

Foundry fork test plan: fork mainnet, deploy 10 NDCs, queue 80 withdrawals via `initiateUnstaking`, measure `gasleft()` before and after `depositETH`, then repeat with 20 NDCs to confirm linear gas growth and identify the revert threshold.
