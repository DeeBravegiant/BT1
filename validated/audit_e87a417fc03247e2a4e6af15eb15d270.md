### Title
`directDeposits` omitted from `produced` value enables unbounded ADA creation - (File: `eras/dijkstra/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Dijkstra/UTxO.hs`)

---

### Summary

In the Dijkstra era, the `directDeposits` field of a transaction body is applied to account balances via `applyDirectDeposits` inside the `ENTITIES` rule, but is **not included** in the `produced` side of the preservation-of-value check. Because neither `consumed` nor `produced` accounts for `directDeposits`, the balance check passes unconditionally regardless of the deposit amount, and the ADA credited to accounts is created from nothing.

---

### Finding Description

The Cardano ledger enforces the **preservation of value** by requiring `consumed == produced` for every valid transaction. For the Dijkstra era the two sides are computed by:

**`getConsumedDijkstraValue`** (`eras/dijkstra/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Dijkstra/UTxO.hs`, lines 65–91):

```haskell
getConsumedDijkstraValue pp lookupStakingDeposit lookupDRepDeposit utxo txBody =
  withBothTxLevels txBody
    (\topTxBody -> txBodyConsumedValue topTxBody <> subTransactionsConsumedValue topTxBody)
    txBodyConsumedValue
  where
    txBodyConsumedValue = getConsumedMaryValue pp lookupStakingDeposit lookupDRepDeposit utxo
```

`getConsumedMaryValue` sums: UTxO inputs + withdrawals + deposit refunds + minted tokens. `directDeposits` is absent.

**`dijkstraProducedValue`** (`eras/dijkstra/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Dijkstra/UTxO.hs`, lines 93–106):

```haskell
dijkstraProducedValue pp isRegPoolId txBody =
  conwayProducedValue pp isRegPoolId txBody          -- outputs + fee + cert-deposits + treasuryDonation
    <> foldMap' (getProducedValue pp isRegPoolId . view bodyTxL)
                (txBody ^. subTransactionsTxBodyL)
```

`conwayProducedValue` sums: outputs + fee + certificate deposits + treasury donation. `directDeposits` is absent.

**`dijkstraSubTxProducedValue`** (lines 252–261) for sub-transactions also omits `directDeposits`:

```haskell
dijkstraSubTxProducedValue pp isRegPoolId txBody =
  sumAllValue (txBody ^. outputsTxBodyL)
    <> inject (getTotalDepositsTxBody pp isRegPoolId txBody <> txBody ^. treasuryDonationTxBodyL)
    <> burnedMultiAssets txBody
```

The preservation-of-value check in `dijkstraUtxoTransition` (`eras/dijkstra/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Dijkstra/Rules/Utxo.hs`, line 381):

```haskell
{- consumed pp utxo₀ txb = produced pp certState txb -}
runTest $ Shelley.validateValueNotConservedUTxO pp originalUtxo certState txBody
```

passes even when `directDeposits = D > 0`, because `D` appears in neither side.

Meanwhile, the `ENTITIES` rule (`eras/dijkstra/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Dijkstra/Rules/Entities.hs`, lines 211–216) unconditionally applies the deposits:

```haskell
let directDeposits = tx ^. bodyTxL . directDepositsTxBodyL
    accountsAfterCerts = certStateAfterCerts ^. certDStateL . accountsL
failOnJust (directDepositsMissingAccounts directDeposits accountsAfterCerts) $
  injectFailure . DirectDepositsToMissingAccounts
pure $ certStateAfterCerts & certDStateL . accountsL %~ applyDirectDeposits directDeposits
```

The only guard is that target accounts are registered (`directDepositsMissingAccounts`). There is no check that the deposited amount is funded by the transaction inputs.

**Contrast with analogous fields that are correctly accounted for:**
- `treasuryDonation` is included in `conwayProducedValue` (`eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/UTxO.hs`, line 131): `<+> inject (txBody ^. treasuryDonationTxBodyL)`
- Certificate deposits are included via `getTotalDepositsTxBody`
- Withdrawals are included in `consumed` via `getConsumedMaryValue`

`directDeposits` is the only value-moving field that is applied to ledger state but excluded from both sides of the balance equation.

---

### Impact Explanation

**Critical — Direct creation of ADA through an invalid ledger state transition.**

An attacker who controls one or more registered stake accounts can submit a Dijkstra-era transaction that credits an arbitrary amount of ADA to those accounts without providing any corresponding UTxO inputs to fund the credit. Because the preservation-of-value check does not include `directDeposits`, the transaction is accepted as valid. The total ADA in the system increases by the sum of all `directDeposits` amounts in every such transaction, violating the fixed-supply invariant of the protocol. This constitutes a direct, unbounded creation of ADA through an invalid ledger state transition.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**High.** The `directDeposits` field is a standard, user-settable field in the `DijkstraTxBody`. Any transaction author can populate it with arbitrary amounts targeting any registered account. No privileged role, governance majority, or special key is required. The only prerequisite is that the target account addresses are registered, which is itself a permissionless operation. The attack path is therefore fully reachable by an unprivileged transaction sender.

---

### Recommendation

Include the sum of `directDeposits` in the `produced` side of the preservation-of-value calculation, mirroring how `treasuryDonation` and certificate deposits are handled:

```haskell
-- In dijkstraProducedValue / dijkstraSubTxProducedValue:
<> inject (fold . unDirectDeposits $ txBody ^. directDepositsTxBodyL)
```

This ensures that any ADA credited to accounts via `directDeposits` must be funded by the transaction's UTxO inputs, restoring the preservation-of-value invariant.
